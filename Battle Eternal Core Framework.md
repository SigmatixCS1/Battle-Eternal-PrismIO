# 🧩 SECTION 1 — THE PRIMARY FAILURE MODE

## ❌ VIBE CODING (DO NOT DO THIS)

**Definition:**  
Creating content based on what “sounds cool” instead of what is **true to the system**.

### Examples of Vibe Coding:
- "Welcome to Chaos"
- "The Hidden War Revealed"
- "Ancient beasts escaped the Fourth Dimension"
- "Join the resistance"
- "Exclusive perks for a select few"

These are not Battle Eternal.  
These are **AI-averaged sci-fi/fantasy phrases**.

---

## ⚠️ Why Vibe Coding Fails

- Replaces **meaning with aesthetic**
- Creates **false canon**
- Introduces **hallucinated lore**
- Makes the IP feel **generic**

---

## 🔥 Real Example (Observed Issue)

- Invented entities (e.g., “F-Corp”)
- Misrepresented the Fourth Dimension
- Introduced a non-canon “resistance”
- Reduced archetypes into generic “powers”

> This is system-level misalignment.

---

## ✅ RULE

> If a line sounds cool but is not grounded in the Obsidian Vault, it is wrong.

# ⚠️ SECTION 4 — AI DRIFT (CRITICAL)

## Definition

AI Drift is when generated content gradually moves away from the original system.

---

## Signs of Drift

- Increasing vagueness  
- Generic phrasing  
- Loss of specificity  
- Introduction of false concepts  
- “Feels right” but isn’t  

---

## Example Drift Language

- "Age of Chaos"
- "Ancient beasts"
- "Hidden war"

---

## ✅ RULE

> If it could belong to another IP, it does not belong here.

---

# 🎭 SECTION 5 — LANGUAGE GUIDELINES

---

## ❌ DO NOT USE

- Chaos (as identity)
- Resistance (unless canonically introduced)
- Powers (oversimplified framing)
- Perks / Rewards / Exclusive Access
- "Select Few"
- "Join Now"
- "Limited Time"

These are **marketing shortcuts**, not narrative language.

---

## ✅ USE LANGUAGE LIKE

- System  
- Pattern  
- Signal  
- Observation  
- Classification  
- Recognition  
- Architecture  
- Distortion  
- Emergence  

---

## 🔥 TONE RULE

> Battle Eternal speaks with precision, not exaggeration.
