# The Aetherforge Program

## Overview

The **Aetherforge Program** is a classified initiative within **The Aetherion Society** aimed at doing something no House has ever admitted to attempting openly:

> **Reduce spellcraft to code.**

Officially, the program is described in Aetherion internal memos as "a cross‑disciplinary exploration of arcano‑technological interfaces." Unofficially, everyone who matters at Saint Radian understands what is at stake:

- If Aetherion succeeds in fully operationalizing the **[[Aetherforge Spell Synthesis Array]]**, they will own the **standard library of magic**.  
- Every spell cast on campus could, in time, be decomposed into **SpellVectors**, cataloged, and re‑instantiated by hardware alone.  
- The distinction between *mage* and *machine* would erode—and with it, the power bases of several rival Houses and Orders.

Rumors of the project have already begun to destabilize the fragile equilibrium between the societies.

---

## Program Goals

1. **Spell Vectorization**  
   - Capture, decompose, and encode spells into stable, queryable data fragments.

2. **Cross‑House Interoperability**  
   - Prove that sigils from **Obsidian Glyphs**, dream‑techniques from **Astral Chorus**, and embodied rituals from **Crimson Flame** can all be represented in a shared mathematical framework.

3. **Division Engine Integration (Limited)**  
   - Interface SpellVectors with the [[Division Engine]] as a **read‑only** reference layer—officially for anomaly detection, unofficially for potential programmatic deployment.

4. **Prototype Replay**  
   - Develop safe protocols for partial spell reconstruction from data alone.

## Political Fallout & House Tensions

### Order of the Obsidian Glyphs

- **Fear:** Their entire ethos is that truth is written in hidden inscriptions. If Aetherion can bypass glyph mastery and call spells from a **menu**, Glyphs risk becoming a legacy priesthood—honored, but unnecessary.  
- **Reaction:** Senior glyph‑scholars push for joint oversight and demand access to Aetherforge data schemas, insisting that "no symbol should be severed from its scriptural lineage."  
- **Whispers:** Some Obsidian Glyphs apprentices speak openly about sabotaging the Compiler Spine if they ever get near it.

### Astral Chorus

- **Fear:** Dream, empathy, and archetype work depend on **subjective interiority**. Reducing Oneiromancy to vectors feels like vivisection of the soul.  
- **Reaction:** Chorus leadership demands strict consent protocols and argues that dream‑signatures should never be included in SpellVectors.  
- **Whispers:** A faction within the Chorus believes the Aetherforge Program is the first step toward **weaponized therapy**—engineered archetypal triggers delivered via spell stacks.

### Order of the Eternal Sol

- **Fear:** Eternal Sol derives authority from being seen as the **chosen interpreters of divine light**. If Aetherion can reproduce Solar miracles with machinery, it undermines their theology and their monopoly on "approved" miracles.  
- **Reaction:** They lobby Headmaster Finch to classify the Program as a support unit under Sol supervision, framing Aetherforge as "laboratory assistants to the true light."  
- **Whispers:** Some Sol adepts discuss crafting ecclesiastical decrees that declare SpellVectors "soulless imitations"—heresy by code.

### Order of the Crimson Flame

- **Fear:** Their power is rooted in **embodied practice**—dueling, discipline, and martial ritual. Spells that can be called by keystroke threaten to turn warriors into obsolete hardware.  
- **Reaction:** The Crimson Flame argues for strict bans on using Aetherforge outputs in sanctioned duels or field missions.  
- **Whispers:** Elite duelists fear a near future where a non‑combatant with a console can outperform years of training.

### House of Living Sigils

- **Fear:** They specialize in **bio‑symbology** and living glyphs. Aetherforge’s purely mechanical frame risks ignoring the organic dimension entirely.  
- **Reaction:** Some seek collaboration—embedding SpellVectors into grown sigil‑flora—while others view the Program as an act of deep hubris.  
- **Whispers:** There are rumors of experiments where SpellVectors seeded into living moss produce unstable, semi‑sentient spell‑gardens.

### House of the Liminal Veil

- **Fear:** Espionage and shapeshifting depend on asymmetry of information. If spell signatures are logged and searchable, many of their covert rituals become **traceable**.  
- **Reaction:** Veil operatives attempt to infiltrate the Program as "security consultants," ostensibly to help design anti‑abuse safeguards.  
- **Whispers:** Some suspect the Liminal Veil intends to insert **false SpellVectors**—phantom spells that never existed—to poison the dataset.

## Toward a House War

The first reports of Aetherforge test runs—heavily redacted, but unmistakable—spark a series of confrontations:

- A closed‑door summit between Aetherion, Obsidian Glyphs, and Eternal Sol ends with raised voices and a sudden blackout of F‑Link feeds in the conference wing.  
- Crimson Flame duelists begin referring to Aetherion students as "button mages" in the training halls, leading to several near‑brawls.  
- Anonymous leaks on student channels claim that Aetherforge rigs are being installed at other Order academies, suggesting a global rollout.

Behind the scenes, **Headmaster Finch** sees Aetherforge as a potential **Phase II** tool for the Order of the Black Sun’s long‑term plans—so long as it can be controlled. But every House senses that whoever ultimately **owns** Aetherforge will be able to:

- Define what "real" magic is.  
- Decide which spells are permissible, deprecated, or forbidden.  
- Potentially overwrite centuries of tradition with a single software update.

The phrase **"House War"** starts appearing in private correspondence—not as open battle, but as:

> Curriculum vetoes, funding cuts, sabotage campaigns, and data‑poisoning operations centered on who gets to write the new canon of magic.

---

## Grant & Spencer’s Position

Although officially minor figures—a curious engineer and a pattern‑obsessed student—**Grant Sterling** and **Spencer Kordell** occupy a uniquely dangerous vantage point:

- Grant understands both the **hardware** and the emotional stakes; he knows exactly how it feels when a beloved system (like Ashen Gate) is rewritten from above.  
- Spencer sees the Program as another layer of **narrative control**: spells turned into configurable assets subject to the same Iconoclasm tactics he has already mapped in entertainment media.

Some in Aetherion whisper that if a House War does break out over Aetherforge, it will begin not with a duel or a decree—but with a **data leak** authored from a forgotten janitor’s office and a VTuber channel that started as a joke.
