The **Aetherion Society** represents the “engineers” of Saint Radian. Its students are coders, builders, and techno-mystics who design containment systems for beasts and proto-resistance tech.  

**Fraternity — Titan’s Workshop**: an industrial dormitory filled with personal labs, machine scrap, and myth-tech forges.  

- **Occult Specialty:** The **Aetherforge Protocol** — fusion of quantum coding, relic-forging, and machine learning. They believe beasts are networks of symbolic data to be stabilized.  
- **Secret:** Their research is *unwittingly siphoned into F-Link* by the Order, strengthening the enemy’s control nets.  
- **Rivalry:** Endless clashes with **Obsidian Glyphs**, who argue that symbols, not code, are the truest tether.  
- **Uniform Accents:** Blazer seams subtly traced with glowing circuit-like etchings.

## The Aetherforge Program (AURIC - Classified)
Aetherion’s highest-risk internal initiative: **reduce spellcraft to code**.

At the center of the program is the (rumored) **Aetherforge Spell Synthesis Array**—a pipeline for capturing spells, decomposing them into reusable **SpellVectors**, and replaying portions of them via hardware.

### Core Objectives
- **Spell Vectorization:** capture and encode spells into stable, queryable fragments.
- **Cross‑House Interoperability:** represent glyph-systems (Obsidian Glyphs), dream-techniques (Astral Chorus), and embodied rituals (Crimson Flame) in a shared mathematical framework.
- **Limited Division Engine Interface:** officially read-only “anomaly detection”; unofficially a pathway to programmable deployment.
- **Prototype Replay:** reconstruct partial spell effects from data alone under controlled protocols.

### Political Fallout
The program destabilizes every other major society because it threatens to create a **standard library of magic**:
- **Obsidian Glyphs:** fear becoming an honored-but-obsolete priesthood; push for schema oversight; sabotage whispers.
- **Astral Chorus:** rejects vectorizing subjective interiority; demands consent boundaries; fears weaponized archetype triggers.
- **Eternal Sol:** fears solar “miracles” reproduced by machine; lobbies to bring Aetherforge under Sol supervision.
- **Crimson Flame:** fears “button mages” undermining embodied discipline; argues for bans in duels/missions.
- **Living Sigils:** split between collaboration (bio-sigil SpellVectors) and hubris warnings; rumors of semi-sentient spell-gardens.
- **Liminal Veil:** fears spell signatures becoming searchable/traceable; attempts infiltration and dataset poisoning (false SpellVectors).

### Toward a House War
Aetherforge’s early test-runs trigger summits, blackouts, harassment, and leak-warfare. The emerging conflict isn’t open battle—it's **vetoes, funding cuts, sabotage campaigns, and data-poisoning operations** over who gets to write the new canon of magic.

### Narrative Tie-In
In the background, Finch and the Order treat Aetherforge as a potential **Phase II** tool—so long as it can be controlled—making the program both a technological arms race and a narrative-control flashpoint.
