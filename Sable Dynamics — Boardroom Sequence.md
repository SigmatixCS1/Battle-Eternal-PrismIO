# Sable Dynamics — Boardroom Sequence

**Location:** Neo-Osaka Corporate Tower  
**Timeline:** Issue 1–2 Cutaway Scene  
**Purpose:** Introduce Sable Dynamics, Grant Sterling’s invention, and Blackout Protocol.

---

## Scene Overview

This scene establishes the **technocratic antagonist force** operating behind global infrastructure.

While Saint Radian appears to be the central mystery, the audience learns that **another system of power is already moving pieces on the board**.

Grant Sterling has unknowingly created technology that threatens several corporate monopolies.

Sable Dynamics has already decided the outcome.

---

# Scene I — The Boardroom

Executives sat around an obsidian table polished to a mirror sheen.

The skyline of **Neo-Osaka** glowed through the glass walls behind them — a cathedral of steel and glass rising above the Pacific.

Beneath the table’s surface, a digital world map pulsed in crimson light.

Trade routes.

Water pipelines.

Energy grids.

Every artery of the modern world.

All of it mapped.

All of it owned.

The room was silent except for the quiet hum of the display.

A man near the head of the table adjusted his cufflinks — black on black.

He spoke without looking up.

> “Is Mr. Sterling en route to Saint Radian?”

No one turned toward him.

No one needed to.

A silhouette sat at the far end of the table.

The figure remained mostly in shadow, the city lights framing only the outline of a composed posture.

The answer came immediately.

> “Yes.”

> “His transport landed in Neo-Osaka fourteen minutes ago.”

A pause.

> “He is already aboard the maglev to Saint Radian.”

A faint nod from the executive.

> “Excellent.”

The table surface flickered.

A file appeared in the center of the projection.

**STERLING FAMILY — AGRICULTURAL HOLDING**

Four generations of farmland.

Nebraska.

---

# Scene II — The Invention

Another screen activated.

Grain footage rolled across the table.

Nebraska.

An endless horizon of cracked earth.

Drought had carved the soil into broken plates.

In the middle of the field stood something strange.

A machine.

Built from repurposed turbine housings.

Solar panels wired to parts that looked salvaged from tractors older than the internet.

Steam rose gently from its frame.

Then condensation formed in the air.

Water began to flow.

Not pumped.

Not extracted.

Generated.

Atmospheric conversion.

Portable.

Self-sustaining.

Powered by a closed-loop energy cell that Sable Dynamics laboratories had attempted to replicate for three years — each attempt ending in catastrophic instability.

Crop yield simulations began to climb.

Global food scarcity projections dropped.

Agricultural dependency chains collapsed across the digital map.

One executive leaned back slightly.

> “If deployed independently…”

He paused, calculating.

> “…this eliminates three of our agricultural subsidiaries within a decade.”

Another voice followed quietly.

> “He built it in a barn.”

No one laughed.

---

# Scene III — The Invitation

The projection shifted again.

A letter appeared.

Saint Radian Academy invites you to join the next generation of systems architects…

The footage transitioned to a farmhouse kitchen.

Morning sunlight through the window.

Grant Sterling stood at the counter reading the letter.

He read it once.

Then again.

Then a third time.

He smiled — the quiet, disbelieving smile farm kids have when the world suddenly notices them.

He thought they wanted his ideas.

They did.

Just not in the way he believed.

---

# Scene IV — The Profile

Back in the boardroom the projections shifted again.

A psychological profile appeared.

**GRANT STERLING**

High empathy.

Low political suspicion.

Strong ethical center.

Minimal exposure to institutional systems.

Low threat awareness.

One executive studied the report.

> “He believes Saint Radian is a school.”

Another voice responded calmly.

> “For most students, it is.”

---

# Scene V — The Farm

The next slide appeared.

**STERLING FARM**

1,200 acres.

Water rights unregistered.

Infrastructure independent.

Energy self-sustaining.

The first executive steepled his fingers.

> “Diplomatic acquisition is preferred.”

Someone across the table asked the question everyone already knew the answer to.

> “And if the family refuses?”

The silhouette finally leaned forward slightly.

For a moment the light caught part of his face.

Just enough to show a calm expression.

His voice carried no emotion.

> “Then Blackout proceeds to Phase Two.”

No one asked for clarification.

None was necessary.

---

# Scene VI — Blackout Protocol

The room lights dimmed as a classified file opened.

**BLACKOUT PROTOCOL — INTERNAL**

Phase I — Offer partnership.

Phase II — Regulatory suffocation.

Phase III — Induce financial insolvency.

Phase IV — Bloodline termination event classification: accidental.

A rendering of the Sterling farmhouse appeared above the table.

Weathered.

Stubborn.

Built by hands that fixed what they owned instead of replacing it.

Four generations of Sterling.

The executive studying the map spoke quietly.

> “His attendance at Saint Radian gives us temporal leverage.”

> “Distance simplifies negotiations.”

The silhouette folded his hands.

> “We do not require the boy’s death.”

A pause.

> “Only his compliance.”

---

# Scene VII — Departure

Outside the tower, Neo-Osaka glittered like a machine that never slept.

High above the city, a maglev train cut through the night.

Inside sat Grant Sterling.

Backpack beside him.

The letter folded carefully in his hands.

He looked out the window toward the distant lights of Saint Radian.

Hope.

Opportunity.

Discovery.

A future he believed he had earned.

Back in Nebraska, wind moved across the dry cornfields.

For now.
