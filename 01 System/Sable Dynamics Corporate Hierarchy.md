# Sable Dynamics Corporate Hierarchy

### Western Technocratic Power Structure

Sable Dynamics operates as the **primary technological and infrastructure conglomerate across the Western Region (United States and allied economies)**.

Publicly it is known for:

- renewable energy infrastructure
    
- agricultural technology
    
- F-Link neural network systems
    
- global logistics platforms
    

Privately it operates as a **policy-shaping technocracy**, influencing:

- government regulation
    
- economic policy
    
- media narratives
    
- resource monopolies
    

Their philosophy:

> **Control the systems people depend on, and you control the world.**

# Sable Dynamics — Blackout Protocol Playbook

**Classification:** Internal Corporate Directive  
**Organization:** [[Sable Dynamics]]  
**Operational Scope:** Western Infrastructure Control Systems  
**Narrative Arc:** Issues 3–12

---

# Overview

**Blackout Protocol** is Sable Dynamics’ covert system for neutralizing individuals, organizations, or technologies that threaten corporate infrastructure monopolies.

Unlike overt corporate litigation or acquisition strategies, Blackout Protocol is designed to **collapse resistance without appearing intentional**.

Targets rarely understand what is happening until the system has already closed around them.

The objective is not simply elimination.

The objective is **compliance or disappearance**.

---

# Strategic Philosophy

Sable Dynamics operates under a foundational belief:

> Infrastructure is power.

Energy grids.

Water systems.

Food logistics.

Information networks.

Any technology that allows people to **operate outside these systems** is considered a destabilizing threat.

Blackout Protocol exists to ensure **no independent system can grow large enough to challenge Sable control**.

---

# Target: Sterling Farm

**Primary Threat:**  
Atmospheric Water Generation Device developed by [[Grant Sterling]]

Capabilities:

- autonomous water production
    
- decentralized agricultural viability
    
- independence from corporate water infrastructure
    

Projected consequences if deployed globally:

- collapse of Sable water distribution contracts
    
- agricultural independence in drought regions
    
- destabilization of global food dependency chains
    

Threat classification:

**Systemic Disruption Event**

---

# Operational Phases

Blackout Protocol operates through four escalating stages.

Targets rarely survive beyond Phase III without surrender.

---

# Phase I — Diplomatic Acquisition

Objective:

Acquire the technology through **voluntary partnership or purchase**.

Tactics include:

- venture capital investment offers
    
- licensing agreements
    
- research partnerships
    
- recruitment of the inventor
    

Psychological approach:

Convince the target they are **joining something larger than themselves**.

Grant’s invitation to [[Saint Radian]] occurs during this phase.

It removes him from the farm while negotiations begin.

---

# Phase II — Regulatory Suffocation

If acquisition fails, the next step is legal containment.

Tactics:

- environmental compliance investigations
    
- zoning disputes
    
- agricultural licensing reviews
    
- water rights litigation
    

The objective is to bury the target under **bureaucratic paralysis**.

Farm operations slow.

Financial strain begins.

Public perception shifts from admiration to suspicion.

---

# Phase III — Economic Collapse

If regulatory pressure fails, Sable deploys financial warfare.

Methods include:

- loan recalls through partner banks
    
- insurance policy cancellations
    
- supply chain disruptions
    
- equipment leasing termination
    

Simultaneously, market analysts publish reports claiming the invention is **economically dangerous**.

Investment groups withdraw support.

The target becomes financially isolated.

---

# Phase IV — Terminal Resolution

Used only when previous phases fail.

Targets are neutralized through **plausible accident scenarios**.

Examples:

- infrastructure failures
    
- vehicle accidents
    
- environmental disasters
    

These events are classified internally as:

**Bloodline Termination Events**

Official cause remains accidental.

No corporate connection is traceable.

---

# Psychological Isolation of Grant Sterling

While Grant studies at [[Saint Radian]], Sable initiates emotional monitoring.

His communications are intercepted through **AI voice synthesis systems**.

When Grant calls home, he hears his family.

A machine replicates their voices using archived recordings.

Purpose:

- monitor Grant’s emotional state
    
- prevent him from returning to Nebraska prematurely
    
- maintain psychological stability while the farm collapses
    

Grant does not initially realize the deception.
