## Scene 3 — Spencer

Location: F-Ride / Streets of Neo-Osaka

---

### Opening Image

Rain-slick Neo-Osaka streets reflected in the ride window.

---

### Script (Tightened VN Version)

**Narrator**

The city slid past the window in broken reflections.

Headlights.  
Rain.  
Surveillance glass.

All of it smeared into something unreadable.

Spencer didn't blink.

---

The moment at the terminal replayed again in his mind.

Not the explosion.

The second before it.

The hesitation.

---

Systems didn't hesitate unless they were resolving a contradiction.

Someone had written a rule.

And something had walked straight through it.

---

_That wasn't an accident._

Spencer’s eyes narrowed.

Accidents scattered.

This had converged.

---

Whatever happened at the check-in counter had been meant to end cleanly.

But when it didn’t…

The machine paid the price instead.

---

Spencer pressed his fingers to his temple and breathed slowly.

His father used to do the same thing after long shifts.

Slow breath in.

Slower breath out.

Panic was noise.

Noise ruined pattern recognition.

---

The boy in the hoodie surfaced again in his mind.

No flinch.

No shock.

No scramble for cover.

Just patience.

---

That calm wasn't bravery.

It was familiarity.

---

_He’s been through this before._

The realization tightened Spencer’s stomach.

---

If someone had tried to erase him through infrastructure…

Then the Order wasn't just watching anomalies.

They were testing methods.

Refining tools.

---

And Spencer had just witnessed a failed prototype.

---

The F-Ride slowed as traffic thickened.

Red brake lights stacked in the rain like evidence markers.

Spencer straightened.

Exhaustion gave way to focus.

---

This wasn't random violence.

It was procedural.

An unseen author nudging the system.

Gauging reactions.

Logging results.

---

_Which means there's a pattern._

Spencer watched the city through the glass.

Patterns always left residue.

---

Whoever _they_ were…

They’d made one mistake.

They’d let a witness walk away.

---

Spencer’s mind began assembling its first board.

Saint Radian wasn't just a school anymore.

It was the next crime scene.

And this time—

He’d be ready when the story tried to lie.

---

### The Spiral Begins

The thought arrived quietly.

Uninvited.

Slippery.

---

_What if it wasn't random at all?_

---

Rain bent the city into something watchful.

They didn't attack the system because it was convenient.

They attacked it because someone knew too much.

Because someone had crossed a threshold without realizing it.

---

Spencer’s chest tightened.

He replayed the terminal again.

Where he had been standing.

What he had been watching.

---

_Was I already on a list?_

---

If the machine was the weapon…

Witnesses were collateral.

And collateral was always acceptable.

---

_What if the failure wasn't the point?_

---

Spencer’s reflection stared back from the glass like an accomplice.

Maybe the explosion was a test.

Stress the network.

See who panics.

See who leaves.

See who doesn’t.

---

His mind built new versions of himself.

One where he stayed.

One where he boarded the train.

One where he sat quietly while something invisible closed around the car.

---

_Would I have vanished between stations?_

His stomach dropped.

---

Or worse.

Would he still be there.

Alive.

Tagged.

Rewritten.

---

### Imagination Spiral

The thought slipped in, quiet and poisonous.

---

The train lurching.

A violent shudder through the carriage.

Lights flickering as the system tried—and failed—to correct itself.

---

Spencer saw it too clearly.

A cascade starting somewhere unseen.

The same way the terminal died.

Logic folding in on itself.

Safeguards tripping too late.

---

_That's what it would've been._

Not an explosion.

Something worse.

---

A controlled failure.

Unfolding slowly enough that no one could stop it.

---

Then the scene tore open.

Gravity went wrong.

Bodies slammed forward as the train screamed along the track.

Passengers thrown like unsecured data.

Faces frozen in disbelief.

---

Screams layered over alarms.

Over tearing metal.

Over the howl of rushing air.

---

Spencer’s pulse spiked as his mind added detail after detail.

Too vivid.

Too specific.

---

_They're still on it._

Horror bloomed in his chest.

Whatever was meant to happen…

Was happening right now.

---

He squeezed his eyes shut.

But the image didn’t fade.

Without evidence, his imagination had seized control.

And it had no interest in restraint.

---

### Reality Returns

A voice cut cleanly through the wreckage.

---

"Hey sweetheart… you alright back there?"

---

The screams collapsed instantly.

Spencer blinked.

The train was gone.

The alarms were gone.

---

Only the low hum of the F-Ride remained.

---

He looked up.

The driver watched him through the mirror.

A small silver-haired woman.

Oversized glasses.

Concern etched into her face.

---

For a moment—

Relief flooded him.

Reality.

Just a ride.

Just a driver.

---

She smiled gently and reached beside her seat.

"You don't look so good," she said.

"I've got some water if you want it."

---

### The Paranoia Returns

The relief didn't last.

---

_Why ask that?_

---

Spencer replayed the sentence instantly.

Tone.

Timing.

Intent.

---

_You don't look so good._

How would she know that unless she’d been watching him?

Unless she’d noticed too much.

---

The city lights flared behind her like a halo.

Or a signal.

---

_What if she's part of it?_

---

Couriers.

Observers.

Cleanup.

That’s how organizations worked.

They never looked like threats.

They looked like grandmothers.

---

She held out the bottle.

Plastic catching the neon light.

Water.

Clear.

Innocent.

---

Spencer stared at it like it was a weapon.

---

_Too convenient._

His mind supplied the next image.

Something colorless dissolving on his tongue.

Witness removed.

Case closed.

---

His throat went dry anyway.

---

If they couldn’t get him at the Nexus…

Maybe this was phase two.

Slow.

Subtle.

Plausibly deniable.

---

He forced himself not to recoil.

Sweat prickled along his neck.

---

There was only patience in her eyes.

Kindness.

---

And somehow—

That made it worse.

---

### The Escape

Spencer’s jaw tightened.

He shrugged his jacket from his shoulders slowly.

Measured movements.

---

_They think they've got me._

---

An old woman.

A bottle of water.

Clean.

Elegant.

---

Cold certainty settled in his chest.

---

_I see the play._

_I always see the play._

---

If this was where they planned to end him…

They’d made one mistake.

They’d given him time to choose how it went down.

---

The door flew open.

Rain and neon exploded into the cabin.

---

Spencer hurled himself sideways.

Hit the pavement.

Rolled once.

Twice.

Then sprang up and ran.

---

Shouts erupted behind him.

He didn't stop.

Didn't think.

Didn't doubt.

---

Just ran.

---

Crowds blurred.

Alleys swallowed him.

The city closed behind him like a sealed file.

---

Minutes later—

Miles away—

He finally slowed.

---

Saint Radian rose ahead.

Its distant spires cut into the night.

---

_I'll be safe there._

Schools had rules.

Structure.

Predictability.

---

If the world was hunting him—

That was the last place it would expect him to hide.

---

Spencer glanced back once.

Scanning the rain-soaked street.

Nothing.

No F-Ride.

No silver hair.

No concerned eyes.

---

Satisfied, he turned forward.

Adjusted his strap.

A thin smile crossed his face.

---

_Nice try, lady._

---

Somewhere behind him—

An elderly driver wondered why her passenger had suddenly vanished into the rain.

---

And Spencer Kordell—

Brilliant.

Shaken.

Certain.

---

Walked toward Saint Radian.

Convinced he had just outsmarted something very dangerous.
