### Preparing for Your Battle Eternal Character Architect Session

A Pre‑Planning Guide for Studio and Writing Teams

### 1. Core Assets to Bring

To make the most of the session, have these items ready (if available):

Battle Eternal IP Spine

IP Bible (universe overview, cosmology, factions, power systems, themes, tone).

Character Bible (Alexander, Castor, Crystalis, DeMarco, Grant, Spencer, Nyx, extended cast).

Season and Episode Materials

Season 1 bible and treatment (The Cognitive Dissonance Arc, 14‑day structure, 12 episodes).

Any existing episode beat sheets, storyboards, or VN scripts.

Visual and Performance References

Concept art, moodboards, or key frames that feel “definitive” for each of the 12 focus characters.

VO references or temp reads for the narrator voice and any core roles.

Existing Writing Artifacts

Past drafts of scenes, dialogue, or monologues that feel on‑model or off‑model.

Any prior tone documents, world notes, or “do not cross” guidelines.

### 2. Strategic Questions to Prepare For (Per Character ICP)

We will move step by step through the Character System Architect blueprint for each of the 12 characters, plus the narrator voice.

Step 1: Character Identity & System Role

What function does this character serve in the Battle Eternal system?

(e.g., Intuitive Rebel, Dormant Judge, Strategic Queen, Tragic Architect, Builder, Evidence Keeper, Coded Rebel).

What is their divine mantle or metaphysical linkage, if any?

(Helios, Nemesis, Athena, Hephaestus, purely human, etc.).

What are their 3–5 defining traits as a persona?

(e.g., sardonic, rigid, analytical, compassionate, haunted).

Step 2: Psychology, Wound, and Desire

What is this character’s core wound?

What do they want on the surface vs. what they truly need?

What is their primary flaw or “failure mode” under stress?

How does their psychology evolve across Season 1 (and beyond, if already mapped)?

Step 3: Voice, Tone, and Emotional Positioning

How does this character sound in dialogue vs. interior thought vs. narrator description?

Which tones fit them by default (e.g., cold, wry, measured, fervent)? Which tones are “rare events”?

Are there specific words, phrases, or metaphors this character consistently uses or avoids?

Step 4: Canon, Power, and Knowledge Constraints

What can this character do by Season 1 (powers, skills, influence) and what is off‑limits until later arcs?

What do they know and not know at each major event (Centaur incident, F‑Link Symposium, Quantum Fracture, Judgment Day)?

Which behaviors or outcomes would break canon for this character unless a deliberate turning point is written?

Step 5: Story Function Across Layers

In the IP Spine: what about this character is permanent vs. allowed to change?

In Season 1: what is their emotional and thematic job in the Cognitive Dissonance Arc?

At the Episode level: what must they achieve or reveal in key episodes where they are foregrounded?

### 3. Channels & Adaptations (How Characters Show Up In Different Media)

We will define how each character’s ICP and voice adapt across the Battle Eternal media stack.

Visual Novel

Dialogue style, choice structures, internal monologue rules, and player‑facing information limits.

TCG / Product Text

How their essence appears in card titles, flavor text, and rule snippets without breaking canon.

Mobile / Live Ops / Community

How the character appears in live events, community posts, and in‑world announcements.

Transmedia (future: anime, books, etc.)

Non‑negotiable voice pillars that must hold regardless of format.

### 4. Optional Prep Work (Highly Recommended)

Before the session, ask your team to:

Review existing scenes for each character and mark:

Which scenes feel “this is canonically them” vs. “this feels off.”

Note any contradictions you have already felt in tone, power level, or ethics.

Identify 1–2 characters where you most fear “AI drift” and want the strictest guardrails.

Think about future arcs (Judgment Day, Crimson Convergence, etc.) and how these characters must be protected to get there.

### 6. Reflection & Save Points

At each step, you will be asked:

“Does this reflect this character fully, or would you like to adjust before moving forward?”

This ensures your character inputs are preserved exactly as stated, with no summarization or reinterpretation by the system.

### 7. Pro Tip For Studio Teams

Treat this like character therapy for your universe.

The clearer and more honest you are about your characters’ wounds, contradictions, and long‑term destinies, the more precise and actionable your Character Architect blueprints will be.

### 8. Pro Tip: Use a Secondary LLM Window For Research & Drafting

To get the most out of your Battle Eternal Character Architect sessions, we recommend opening a second AI workspace or LLM window alongside your main working session.

Why?

The main Character Architect window is where you will go step by step through the modules that define each character’s ICP and canon rules.

The secondary window is your scratchpad to explore ideas, test alt versions of answers, and refine thinking before you commit it.

How to Use It:

Open a second LLM/chat window (Perplexity, GPT, Claude, or any other AI tool you prefer).

Use that window to:

Brainstorm answers to upcoming questions for each character.

Research psychological patterns, archetypes, and narrative structures you might want to apply.

Draft and refine your answers in plain language until they feel true to the IP.

Once you are satisfied, copy and paste your final answer into the Character Architect working session window.

Capturing Your Results:

After each character module or sub‑module, copy the finalized results into Notion or your IP knowledge system.

Over time, this builds a governed, searchable character library that plugs directly into the Battle Eternal IP Production Map.

This dual‑window approach keeps your official blueprint session clean, while giving writers and producers space to think, experiment, and align before locking canon for the AI to follow.
