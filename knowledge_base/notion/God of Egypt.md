# God of Egypt

Gods of Egypt flopped due to a combination of issues, primarily its widely criticized whitewashing casting of white actors as Egyptian gods, poor and inconsistent visual effects (CGI), a convoluted plot with underdeveloped characters, and generally terrible reviews, creating bad buzz and failing to attract audiences despite its large budget. The movie failed to generate excitement and was seen as a misguided attempt at a blockbuster fantasy, lacking depth and originality, leading to significant financial losses.

Key Reasons for Failure:

Whitewashing Controversy: The casting of predominantly white actors (like Gerard Butler and Nikolaj Coster-Waldau) to play ancient Egyptian deities sparked immediate backlash and negative press before the film even released, which it couldn't overcome.

[Poor Visuals](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=24917e94988db5c1&cs=1&sxsrf=ANbL-n6Ze5-ys7kL3ioKJHXXUw6xfulkUw:1770252771744&q=Poor+Visuals&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiciPy8kcGSAxV3le4BHe44B2IQxccNegQIGhAB): Many critics and viewers found the special effects unconvincing, with bad green screen work and video-game-like CGI that didn't hold up on the big screen, even for 3D showings.

Weak Story & Characters: The plot was seen as messy and episodic, with characters lacking depth, motivation, and emotional connection for the audience, despite the presence of star power like [Chadwick Boseman](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=24917e94988db5c1&cs=1&sxsrf=ANbL-n6Ze5-ys7kL3ioKJHXXUw6xfulkUw:1770252771744&q=Chadwick+Boseman&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiciPy8kcGSAxV3le4BHe44B2IQxccNegQIKhAB).

Scathing Reviews & Low Audience Scores: The film received extremely poor critical reviews (around 15% on Rotten Tomatoes) and low audience scores (B- CinemaScore), indicating a lack of connection with viewers.

Failed to Stand Out: In a crowded market, it didn't offer a fresh take on the fantasy genre, feeling generic and failing to capture the cultural relevance of successful franchises like Marvel or even older mythological epics.

Budget vs. Return: With a $140 million budget, its meager $38 million worldwide gross made it a significant box office bomb, losing the studio millions.
