#### Mythology Differentiation Opportunity

Original Assessment: Greek + Japanese god collision is underserved

Revised Assessment: CONFIRMED as white-space opportunity, BUT requires explanation threshold

The research validated that mythology-grounded VNs are rare in Western market. However, new insight:

The ICP needs to understand why the mythology matters within the first 30 seconds of seeing the game, or they categorize it as "aesthetic theming" like dozens of other "gods/mythology-inspired" games that use it superficially. The differentiation only works if:

Character psychology stems from mythological nature (not just god names on anime characters)

Story beats require mythological knowledge to fully appreciate (rewards lore-deep players)

Theological conflicts drive plot (not generic good vs evil)

ICP Implication: Marketing must lead with "theological psychology" angle, not just "Greek + Japanese gods" aesthetic. Example: "What happens when Zeus's authority complex collides with Amaterasu's duty-bound honor?" vs "Gods from two mythologies battle in Neo-Tokyo."

Episodic releases that create cliffhangers and unanswered mythology questions become social currency the ICP uses to bond with others.

### HOW TO SPEAK TO THEM

#### Voice & Tone Guidelines

DO Use:

Mythology-grounded specificity: "Zeus's authority complex collides with Amaterasu's duty-bound honor" not "gods battle"

Emotional truth: "You won't be able to save everyone" not "epic choices"

Transparency: "Episode 1 free, Episodes 2-6 $9.99 each, 6 total planned" not vague "episodic series"

Community invitation: "Join Discord to theorize" not "follow our social media"

Calm confidence: "90% Episode 1 complete, releasing Q2 2026" not "coming soon!"

DO NOT Use:

Generic hype language: "epic," "unmissable," "revolutionary"

Marketing superlatives without proof: "most immersive VN ever"

Fake urgency: "limited time offer," "early bird pricing"

Corporate speak: "leveraging IP," "transmedia ecosystem"

Comparisons claiming superiority: "better than Steins;Gate"

#### Messaging Frameworks

Mythology Positioning:
Weak: "Greek and Japanese gods in Neo-Tokyo"

Strong: "When Zeus's need to dominate meets Amaterasu's sacred duty to protect, who survives the theological war?"

Episodic Completion Promise:
Weak: "New episodes coming soon!"

Strong: "Episode 1 complete. Episode 2: 60% done, releasing July 2026. Full 6-episode series roadmap in Discord."

Choice Consequence Proof:
Weak: "Your choices shape the story"

Strong: "Route A: Save Zeus, lose Amaterasu's trust. Route B: Ally with Amaterasu, face Zeus's wrath. Genuinely divergent outcomes, not cosmetic changes."

Free Entry Invitation:
Weak: "Try our demo!"

Strong: "Episode 1 is free. 2-3 hours. If you don't fall in love with the mythology, you risk nothing. If you do, Episodes 2-6 are $9.99 each."

### ANTI-PATTERNS (What NOT to Do)

#### Marketing Anti-Patterns

Launch paid Episode 1 before building audience

→ Triggers: "Pay before proving you can finish" skepticism

Kickstarter without playable demo

→ Triggers: "Another crowdfunding scam" assumption

Vague "coming soon" without dates

→ Triggers: "Vaporware" or "they don't have a plan" doubt

Marketing to "anime fans" broadly

→ Wrong ICP: This is VN enthusiast, not casual anime watcher

Paid social media ads as primary discovery

→ ICP ignores ads; trusts community recommendations only

#### Content Anti-Patterns

Mythology as flavor text, not plot driver

→ Triggers: "Shallow theming" objection immediately

Episodic cliffhangers without community engagement space

→ Wastes theory-crafting potential; no social stickiness

Static sprites with "animated" label

→ Destroys trust; premium positioning collapses

Fake branching (cosmetic choice variations)

→ ICP replays, discovers deception, refunds + negative review

## Persona Snapshot (Primary ICP)

The Mythology Seeker discovers new visual novels almost entirely through trusted humans—VTuber reactions, Reddit threads with screenshots, or Discord recommendations—because algorithmic ads and Steam discovery feel noisy and untrustworthy. ​

They maintain a large backlog, so time is more precious than money; every new VN competes against classics they already trust. ​

Before buying, they look for proof signals: a free Episode 1, visible production quality, and signs the creators will actually finish the story. ​

They emotionally invest quickly once a character or mythological mystery “clicks,” but will disengage permanently if the story feels shallow, misleading, or abandoned. ​

After finishing Episode 1, they seek community spaces to compare interpretations, theorize about gods’ motives, and validate that others noticed the same mythological details. ​

Purchase of Episode 2 is triggered not by hype, but by confidence: confidence in completion, confidence that choices matter, and confidence that the mythology is doing real psychological work. ​
