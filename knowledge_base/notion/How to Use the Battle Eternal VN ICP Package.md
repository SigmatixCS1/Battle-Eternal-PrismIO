# How to Use the Battle Eternal VN ICP Package

This kit is your operating system for Battle Eternal’s “Visual Novel ICP”. It tells you who you are building for, how they behave, how to price, and how to launch in a way that actually matches their psychology.

Think of it in four layers:

ICP Persona

ICP Playbook

Pricing Strategy

Research & GTM Analysis

Use them in this order.

### 1. Start Here: Mythology Seeker ICP Persona

File:

Mythology\_Seeker\_ICP\_Persona\_Visual\_Novel\_Buyer

Audience: Everyone (must‑read once), especially writers, marketers, and art leads.

Purpose: Define exactly who the VN is for and how they think.

How to read:

Read pages 1–3 slowly. Highlight:

Age, identity, backlog reality, discovery habits.

Emotional and functional wins (mythology depth, completion confidence, community belonging).

Read the “Mythology Differentiation” and “Community Participation” sections; these explain why theological psychology and theory‑crafting are non‑negotiable.

Skim the Itch.io Culture ICP section to understand why itch.io is a separate but important segment.

How to execute:

Before creating any VN‑facing asset (Steam page, trailer, Discord post, character art), answer the five questions on page 1:

Does this prove completion viability?

Does this demonstrate mythology depth?

Does this invite community participation?

Does this remove risk?

Does this speak human truth vs hype?

If you cannot answer “yes” to these, the asset is not yet ICP‑aligned.

### 2. Operational Core: Visual Novel ICP Playbook

File:

Visual\_Novel\_ICP\_Playbook

Audience: Leadership, GTM, narrative, art, production.

Purpose: Turn the persona and research into concrete rules, priorities, and metrics.

How to read:

Page 1–2: Executive Summary + Non‑Negotiable Truths

Lock in the five non‑negotiables (Ep1 free, completion confidence, community as cognitive driver, mythology→psychology, time ROI). These govern all tradeoffs.

Page 3–5: ICP Snapshot + Drivers Map

Confirm behavioral journey and psychological drivers so your decisions match real behavior.

Page 6–7: Objections, Triggers, Guardrails

Note the Objection Heatmap and Conversion Triggers. These directly inform store copy, trailers, and community posts.

Page 8–9: How Each Team Uses This ICP + Metrics

Each function gets clear marching orders.

Metrics section tells you what to track (downloads, completion, conversion, community activity) and reminds you these are targets, not promises.

How to execute:

Leadership: Use Non‑Negotiables and “Strategic Implications for GTM” as the default answer key for debates (e.g., “Ep1 free?” “Kickstarter now?” “Paid ads?”).

Marketing: Build channel plan, Steam page, trailer, and VTuber outreach directly from the GTM, Objection Heatmap, and Conversion Triggers sections.

Narrative: Use the Drivers Map and Mythology rules to shape Episode 1 structure, choices, and character arcs.

Art: Use mythology→psychology and visual prompts (via the persona and character docs) to design characters and scenes that convert, not just look cool.

Analytics / Production: Implement the metrics as your dashboard; treat deviations as triggers to revisit creative/GTM, not pricing first.

### 3. Pricing and Monetization: Episode 1 Pricing Strategy

File:

EPISODE\_1\_PRICING\_STRATEGY

Audience: Leadership, production, finance, GTM.

Purpose: Define how money flows and when to adjust.

How to read:

Read the Final Position section first (last pages):

Episode 1 permanently free.

Season Pass for Episodes 2–6 at $49.99 as target mature‑state price.

Pricing is a hypothesis subject to data.

Then read the Decision Tree:

What to do if downloads are low.

What to do if completion is low.

What to do if Season Pass conversion is low.

When (and only when) to consider changing price.

How to execute:

Treat Episode 1 as acquisition + IP validation, not revenue.

Use Ep1 downloads, completion, and Ep2/Season Pass conversion as your primary signals.

Exhaust discovery, quality, and transparency fixes before touching price.

Only test pricing changes if conversion stays weak after you’ve improved those areas.

The Playbook references this file explicitly under “Pricing Strategy”; think of this as the monetization appendix.

### 4. Evidence and GTM Logic: Market Research + Kickstarter Analysis

Files:

Visual\_Novel\_Buyer\_ICP\_Market\_Research\_Data

IS\_KICKSTARTER\_THE\_RIGHT\_GTM\_FOR\_BATTLE\_ETERNAL\_TRUTH-CALIBRATED\_ANALYSIS

Audience: Leadership, strategy, anyone who wants to understand “why.”

Purpose: Back up the Playbook and pricing with market data and GTM patterns.

How to read:

In the Market Research Data doc, focus on:

Upgraded severity of abandonment fear and time anxiety.

Discovery hierarchy (friends → VTubers → Reddit → Discord → itch.io → Steam → ads).

Reprioritized drivers (completion, community, time ROI).

In the Kickstarter GTM doc, focus on:

Why Kickstarter is a conversion tool for existing fans, not a discovery tool.

Why VN must come first, then TCG, with concrete audience thresholds.

How to execute:

Use these docs to answer “why are we doing this?” for investors, internal skeptics, or yourself.

Do not redesign the ICP or pricing from scratch without checking whether new data truly overturns these foundations.

### 5. Putting it all together (simple sequence)

When planning or revisiting anything VN‑related:

Start with the Persona

Remind yourself who the Mythology Seeker is and what they want.

Check the Playbook

Use Non‑Negotiables, GTM implications, and team‑specific sections to plan your next sprint.

Confirm Pricing and Revenue Logic

Ensure any monetization idea respects: Ep1 free, validation first, pricing as hypothesis.

Reference Research as Needed

When challenged, pull supporting evidence from Market Research + Kickstarter analysis.

Measure, Learn, Update

After Episode 1 and each major update, compare real metrics to Playbook targets.

If behavior diverges, update the Playbook and (if needed) the Persona and Pricing docs, rather than ignoring the gap.
