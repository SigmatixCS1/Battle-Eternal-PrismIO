# Compliance Check for VN ICP

Use at the start of every sprint, launch, or major asset.

### 1. Are we anchored in the Mythology Seeker ICP?

Check:

We have re‑read the Mythology Seeker persona before planning this sprint.

We are targeting: story‑first VN/anime fan, 18–34, with 200+ hour backlog, who discovers via VTubers, Reddit, Discord, and itch.io.

Why this is critical (all stages, all channels):

If you drift toward “generic anime audience” or “general Steam player,” you will design assets and GTM that fight the actual buyer’s behavior, not flow with it. You’ll overspend on ads, under‑invest in community, and misjudge what feels compelling.

### 2. Are we respecting the Non‑Negotiable Truths?

Check (all must be “yes”):

Episode 1 remains permanently free on all platforms.

We are not asking for money before we’ve proven quality and completion capability.

Every plan strengthens completion confidence, not weakens it (roadmaps, devlogs, visible progress).

Mythology is driving character psychology and conflict in our assets, not just aesthetics.

We are designing for time ROI in a saturated backlog world (clear length, strong hook).

Why this is critical:

These truths are the “physics” of your buyer’s psychology. Violating them (for example, charging for Episode 1, downplaying roadmap transparency, or treating gods as labels) directly hits the top reasons they say “no.”

### 3. Does this plan align with the ICP’s discovery paths?

Check:

Primary touchpoints in this sprint: VTubers/YouTube, Reddit, Discord, itch.io. Steam and ads are secondary.

We have a concrete list of creators, communities, and threads we’re targeting.

We are not relying on Steam’s algorithm or paid social as our main discovery engine.

Why this is critical:

Your buyer trusts humans and communities, not algorithms or ads. If you over‑index on algorithmic or paid channels, you will see impressions without conversion and misread that as “game not appealing,” when the real issue is channel mismatch.

### 4. Does every asset pass the 5 ICP questions?

For each major asset (Steam page, trailer, devlog, Discord announcement, key art):

Check:

Does this prove completion viability? (roadmap, episode status, behind‑the‑scenes)

Does this demonstrate mythology depth? (theology → psychology, not god names only)

Does this invite community participation? (questions, theory hooks, Discord link)

Does this remove risk? (free Episode 1, choice proof, clear expectations)

Does this speak human truth vs marketing hype? (no superlatives, no fake urgency)

Why this is critical:

These five checks are the bridge from insight to execution. If an asset fails them, it might look “cool” but will not move your exact buyer to act or talk about the game.

### 5. Are we handling objections and triggers explicitly?

Check:

This sprint’s assets explicitly address:

Abandonment fear (show progress, timelines).

Backlog displacement (“why this instead of Steins;Gate tonight?”).

“Mythology is just aesthetic” and “choices don’t matter.”

We are building at least one Tier‑1 conversion trigger into the sprint (Ep1 free completion path, VTuber emotional run, or a community mystery thread).

Why this is critical:

If you do not actively neutralize core objections, they default to “no,” no matter how good the story is. And if you do not build Tier‑1 triggers, they may like the idea but never take the first step.

### 6. Are GTM and pricing decisions still aligned with the ICP?

Check:

We are not planning any early TCG Kickstarter or large TCG spend before VN audience metrics are met (e.g., 10k+ Ep1 downloads, ~40% completion).

We are not experimenting with Ep1 monetization; all tests focus on discovery, quality, and transparency first.

Any pricing experiment on Episodes 2–6 is scheduled after we have real data on completion and conversion.

Why this is critical:

Big monetization and GTM moves are the easiest way to betray this ICP’s trust. If you move too early on TCG or Ep1 pricing, you undercut the very completion confidence and long‑term fandom you need.

### 7. Do we have measurement and learning baked into this sprint?

Check:

We have explicitly chosen which metrics we expect to move (downloads, completion, Ep2/Season Pass conversion, Discord joins, thread count).

We have a simple plan to review these metrics after the sprint and update the Playbook if they contradict our assumptions.

Someone is responsible for writing a short “what we learned” note tied back to the ICP.

Why this is critical:

Without measurement linked back to the ICP, the system stops being “living infrastructure” and becomes a static deck. Your advantage comes from closing the loop: ICP → plan → behavior → Playbook update.

If every sprint and campaign answers these checks with honest “yes” (or triggers a revision where the answer is “no”), you keep all channels and stages aligned to the Mythology Seeker’s reality instead of drifting back to generic game marketing.
