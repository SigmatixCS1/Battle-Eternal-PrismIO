### Five Non-Negotiables (Governs ALL Tradeoffs)

These override personal preferences, industry norms, and attractive alternatives:

Episode 1 stays permanently free on all platforms

No money requests before proving quality and completion capability

Every decision strengthens completion confidence (roadmaps, devlogs, progress bars)

Mythology drives character psychology, not just aesthetics

Design for time ROI in a saturated backlog world (clear length, strong emotional hook)

If a decision violates these, stop and escalate to leadership.

### Team-Specific Responsibilities

#### Leadership

Your Job: Use the ICP as the "answer key" for all strategic debates

Daily Actions:

Reference Non-Negotiables when resolving conflicts ("Should Ep1 be paid?" → No.)

Use "Strategic Implications for GTM" section for publisher/investor discussions

Protect Episode 1 quality even if it delays secondary initiatives

Gate major GTM moves (TCG Kickstarter) behind VN validation metrics

Decision Authority:

Green-light only if: proves completion viability, demonstrates mythology depth, invites community participation, removes risk, speaks truth vs hype

Target thresholds before external moves: 10K+ Ep1 downloads, 40% completion, 15% Season Pass conversion

#### GTM / Marketing

Your Job: Build discovery and conversion engine aligned to ICP behavior

Priority Discovery Channels (in order):

VTubers & narrative/horror streamers (5K-50K followers)

Reddit (r/visualnovels, r/VNs, mythology subreddits)

Discord community partnerships (mythology/indie VN servers)

itch.io presence (game jams, curation)

Steam algorithm (ONLY after social proof exists)

Paid ads (amplifier only, NOT primary channel)

Messaging Strategy:

Lead with "Free Episode 1 as proof, not promise"

Show roadmap transparency and episode progress

Prove mythology drives psychology through character examples

Explicitly invite community theory-crafting

Before Publishing Anything:
Run the 5-Question Check:

Does this prove completion viability?

Does this demonstrate mythology depth?

Does this invite community participation?

Does this remove risk?

Does this speak human truth vs marketing hype?

Voice Guidelines:

DO: Mythology specificity, emotional truth, transparency, community invitation, calm confidence

AVOID: Generic hype ("epic," "revolutionary"), fake urgency, corporate speak, unsupported comparisons

#### Narrative Team

Your Job: Structure Episode 1 to convert Mythology Seekers

Core Framework:

Use Drivers Map and Mythology rules from Playbook

Shape Episode 1 structure, choices, and character arcs around theological psychology

Mythology must drive character decisions and conflicts, not just provide names/aesthetics

Choice Design:

Every branching choice should reflect underlying philosophies (authority vs empathy vs justice)

Prove "choices matter" through consequences that affect reality fracture

Show completion path clarity (how many episodes, what's the arc)

Quality Bar:

Target 2-3 hour playtime for Episode 1

Aim for emotional attachment within first 30 minutes

Design for 40%+ completion rate

#### Art Team

Your Job: Create "visual theology" that signals depth at first glance

Design Philosophy:

Mythology→Psychology: Art must prove theological depth, not just look cool

Use Character Development Prompts checklist for every asset

Design for transmedia consistency (VN → TCG → Mobile)

Production Workflow:

Phase 1: Concept (Mythology-First)

Identify core mythological archetype

Research divine function (what did this god DO?)

Define psychological burden divine nature creates

Determine visual contradiction (god in human form)

Phase 2: Visual Development (AI-Efficient)

Generate concepts with AI + art director refinement

Validate against 7-category checklist

Test environmental interaction (Neo-Tokyo reaction)

Cross-check transmedia translation

Phase 3: Community Validation

Release teaser assets to Discord/Reddit

Monitor theory-crafting activity

Identify which visual hooks landed

Environment & UI:

Show Neo-Tokyo reacting to divinity (glitching, space bending)

UI should reflect theological philosophies in choice presentation

Include animation proof in every trailer/screenshot batch

#### Analytics / Production

Your Job: Implement metrics dashboard and protect Episode 1 development timeline

Primary Metrics to Track:

Discovery:

Episode 1 downloads (first 90 days)

Discord joins after announcements

VTuber outreach response rate

Engagement:

Episode 1 completion rate (target: 40%+)

Average playtime (target: 2-3 hours)

Community theory posts per week (target: 10+)

Conversion:

Episode 1 → Episode 2 conversion (target: 15-25% within 30 days)

Season Pass purchases

itch.io voluntary tips (target: 5-10% of downloads)

Red Flags:

Downloads <5K in first 30 days = discovery problem

Completion <25% = narrative hook failing

Ep2 conversion <10% = quality/pricing/completion confidence issue

When Metrics Diverge:

Treat as trigger to revisit creative/GTM, NOT pricing first

Update Playbook based on learning

Write "what we learned" note tied back to ICP

### When Things Don't Work

#### If Metrics Underperform:

Discovery Low (<5K downloads in 30 days)

Audit discovery channels (are we in the right places?)

Check VTuber outreach effectiveness

Review messaging clarity

Test itch.io vs Steam positioning

DO NOT adjust pricing yet

Completion Low (<25%)

Audit Episode 1 pacing and emotional attachment timing

Review mythology accessibility (too obscure?)

Check tutorial/onboarding clarity

Test if choices feel meaningful enough

DO NOT adjust pricing yet

Conversion Low (<10%)

Audit completion confidence signals (roadmap clear?)

Review Episode 2 value proposition

Check Season Pass framing

Test if mythology depth was proven in Ep1

Only THEN consider pricing experiments

Critical Rule: Exhaust discovery, quality, and transparency fixes BEFORE touching price.

Quality bar lowered to hit arbitrary deadline

Mythology reduced to aesthetic

Metrics Catastrophically Low

<1K downloads after 30 days

<10% completion rate

<2% conversion rate

Escalation Path:

Flag to your function lead
