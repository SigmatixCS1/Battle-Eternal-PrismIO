# GTM Program for Series 1 Episode 1

PART 0: NORTH STAR AND GUARDRAILS

North Star

Launch Battle Eternal Episode 1 as a free, mythology-driven psychological horror VN on Steam and itch.io, using a Virtual Red Carpet premiere plus a Mythology Seeker aligned influencer program to prove completion, spark theory-crafting, and build a durable community that funds future episodes.

Non‑negotiable constraints

Episode 1 remains permanently free and substantial (complete emotional arc plus cliffhanger).

No crowdfunding or TCG monetization before VN audience and metrics are validated.

GTM must match Mythology Seeker behavior: VTubers, Reddit, Discord, itch.io, and trusted humans over ads.

Primary VN targets (first 6–9 months)

10–25K Episode 1 downloads.

40 percent Episode 1 completion rate.

15–25 percent Ep1 to Episode 2 or Season Pass conversion once paid content exists.

3–5K Discord members and a steady cadence of theory threads and UGC.

PART 1: VIRTUAL RED CARPET PREMIERE AS LAUNCH ANCHOR

Purpose

Create a global, community-first premiere that:

Anchors “mythology as psychology” as the core promise.

Demonstrates completion intent (roadmap, production cadence, transparency).

Invites Mythology Seekers into an ongoing theory-crafting community rather than a one‑night spectacle.

Format

Location: Discord Stage (primary), simulcast to YouTube; optional Twitch simulcast for extra reach.

Duration: around 90 minutes.

Segments:

Red carpet welcome and ICP nod (“for people who treat mythology as psychology”).

Founder and studio story (Hybrid Philosophy, 25K investment, AI‑efficient pipeline).

Mythology and character deep dive (Alexander, DeMarco, Cas; theology research).

Karmic choice and branching system explanation.

Live Q and A curated from Discord and pre-submitted questions.

Episode 1 premiere slice (15–20 minutes of real gameplay).

Short TCG proof-of-concept teaser (three cards, clear VN‑first framing).

Closing CTAs and Discord afterparty.

Success metrics for the event

1,000+ live concurrent viewers across platforms and 3–5K VOD views in week one.

Spike in wishlists and pre-registrations (target 2–5K new wishlists around event window).

Significant Discord growth (target +1–2K members) and 20+ new theory threads in 72 hours.

PART 2: 12‑WEEK CONTENT ROADMAP (SUMMARY)

Phasing (already expanded in your GTM\_Development doc)

Weeks 1–3: Foundation / “Discovery” – founder and studio story, “what is Battle Eternal” gameplay explainer, Discord and email launch.

Weeks 4–6: Mythology Deep Dive / “Education” – theology and character talks, roadmap and completion transparency, karmic choice system showcase.

Weeks 7–9: Community Activation / “Engagement” – theory prompts, feedback loops, fan spotlights, and Virtual Red Carpet announcement and RSVPs.

Weeks 10–12: Launch Momentum / “Conversion” – countdown, event execution, Episode 1 launch, and conversion to downloads and Discord activity.

Each week carries ICP “pass/fail” checks so assets must:

Prove completion viability and roadmap.

Demonstrate mythology depth with concrete examples.

Invite community participation explicitly.

Remove risk (free Ep1, clear length, no surprise monetization).

PART 3: INFLUENCER, PRESS, AND INTERVIEW PROGRAM

Strategic intent

Borrow audiences from creators who already speak to Mythology Seekers, instead of trying to grow a large BE channel before launch.

Use interviews and deep-dive content to frame Battle Eternal as serious myth-psychology, not generic anime horror.

Tiers and roles

Tier S (small number, high leverage)

Large VTubers and essay channels in VN, horror, mythology, or theology.

Primary use: one or two long-form interviews or features, optional premiere watchalong.

Tier A (core engine)

Mid-size VTubers, VN streamers, lore channels, and critics with audiences close to your ICP.

Primary use: co-stream Virtual Red Carpet, Episode 1 full playthroughs, theory videos in launch week and beyond.

Tier B (long tail)

Small VTubers, narrative streamers, indie dev and AI workflows podcasts, and theology/mythology podcasters.

Primary use: founder interviews, behind-the-scenes episodes, slower-burn coverage across the first few months.

Incentive and asset program

Value proposition (what they get)

A mythology-driven, theology-aware VN built for long-form interpretation; ideal for deep-dive content.

Free, substantial Episode 1, making it easy and ethical to recommend.

Public roadmap and progress updates that reduce abandonment risk.

An active Discord community where their audience can immediately participate in theory threads.

Incentives

Non-monetary (default)

Early access to Episode 1 builds or curated segments.

“Founding Seeker” creator role, recognition in Discord and credits.

Exclusive behind-the-scenes materials (concept art, mythology notes, process breakdowns).

Optional revenue mechanics (later, once paid content exists)

Tracked links and revenue share on Season Pass sales driven by their audience.

Time-limited discounts or cosmetic bonuses for their community.

Shared asset folder

Visuals: logos, key art (multiple crops), roadmap image, character one-pagers.

Video: founder story, “What is Battle Eternal” trailer, mythology deep dive, choice system showcase, clean Episode 1 slice.

Docs: one-page fact sheet, press Q and A, spoiler and streaming guidelines.

Outreach timeline (aligned to 12‑week plan)

Weeks 1–3: warm-up and discovery

Soft intros, share unlisted founder video and basic trailer, ask for feedback and fit.

Weeks 4–6: proof sharing

Deliver mythology and completion transparency pieces, plus choice system showcase; ask who is open to future streams or interviews.

Weeks 7–9: concrete offers

Invite co-streaming the Virtual Red Carpet, scheduling interviews, and participating in “Theory Week”.

Weeks 10–12: execution

Finalize schedules, deliver overlays and assets, send keys or builds, and coordinate live coverage of the premiere and Episode 1.

PART 4: CHANNEL MIX AND BUDGET

Channel priorities

Tier 1 channels

Discord (community hub and theory engine).

VTubers / YouTube (borrowed audience, co-streams, essays).

Steam and itch.io (storefronts linked from every major piece).

Tier 2 channels

Reddit (r/visualnovels, r/horror, mythology subreddits).

Email (lore updates, roadmap, theory prompts).

X and Instagram (supporting distribution and identity content).

Tier 3 channels (amplifiers only)

Paid Meta or YouTube ads against well-performing assets, not as core discovery.

Budget principles

Allocate the limited VN marketing budget roughly:

VTuber and creator support (art, overlays, possibly small stipends or bounties): primary share.

Production of a small number of high-quality videos (founder story, trailer, myth deep-dive, choice showcase).

Minimal paid ads reserved for boosting content that already performs well organically, never as the main engine.

PART 5: KPI FRAMEWORK AND DECISION GATES

Pre-launch KPIs (12‑week period)

Creator pipeline: Number of warm relationships by tier, number of confirmed co-streams and interviews.

Audience-building: Discord members, email subscribers, social followers, and wishlists.

Engagement: theory thread count, Q and A participation, clip shares and saves.

Event KPIs

Live and VOD view counts.

Wishlist and Discord join spikes around event date.

Number and depth of community theory threads post-event.

Post-launch VN KPIs (first 90 days after Episode 1)

Episode 1 downloads and completion rate.

Ep1 to paid content conversion once live.

Review volume and rating on Steam and itch.io.

Discord growth and sustained theory-crafting.

Decision use

If Episode 1 downloads and completion exceed targets and community activity is strong, increase spend on VTuber collaborations and proceed toward Episode 2 and Season Pass plans.

If downloads are strong but completion or community engagement is weak, prioritize UX and narrative improvements and community scaffolding before launching paid content.

If downloads are weak despite strong creator and content work, revisit positioning and hooks before making pricing or transmedia changes.
