### Campaign Structure & Timeline

#### The 12-Week Pre-Launch + Launch Model

#### Weeks 1-3: Foundation & Content Production

Focus: Founder story, trailer, asset creation, initial outreach setup

Budget: $2,000-$3,000

Key Activities: Record founder video, create trailer, set up channels

#### Weeks 4-6: Creator Outreach & Proof Sharing

Focus: Mythology transparency, completion promise, creator relationship building

Budget: $1,500-$2,500 (triggered by 5+ positive creator responses)

Key Activities: Send creator outreach, share BTS content, Discord seeding

#### Weeks 7-9: Engagement & Event Prep

Focus: Virtual Red Carpet planning, co-streamer commitments, community events

Budget: $2,000-$3,000 (triggered by 10+ co-streamer confirmations)

Key Activities: Lock co-streamers, create overlays, theory contests

#### Weeks 10-12: Launch Sprint

Focus: Virtual Red Carpet execution, Episode 1 launch, early reviews

Budget: $2,500-$3,500

Key Activities: Execute premiere, launch on Steam/itch.io, seed reviews

#### Weeks 13-16: Post-Launch Retention

Focus: Convert completers to Season Pass, sustain community, optimize funnel

Budget: Reallocate from underperforming channels

Key Activities: Email nurture, Discord events, creator follow-ups

### The Math - Three Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Conservative (Prepare for This)

Probability: 60%

Ep1 Downloads: 2,000 (30 days)

Completion Rate: 30%

Completers: 600

Season Pass Conversion: 6%

Season Pass Buyers: 36

Gross Revenue: $1,800 ($49.99 × 36)

Net Revenue: $1,260 (after Steam 30% cut)

CAC: $333/buyer ($12K ÷ 36)

LTV:CAC: 0.15:1 (not viable)

Outcome: Phase 1 fails to validate model. Need to diagnose: Was it discovery (too few downloads)? Quality (low completion)? Pricing/value perception (low conversion)?

#### Scenario 2: Base Case (Plan for This)

Probability: 30%

Ep1 Downloads: 5,000 (mostly organic from VTuber coverage)

Completion Rate: 40%

Completers: 2,000

Season Pass Conversion: 10%

Season Pass Buyers: 200

Gross Revenue: $10,000

Net Revenue: $7,000

CAC: $60/buyer ($12K ÷ 200)

LTV:CAC: 0.83:1 (still not sustainable)

Outcome: Proves model works at small scale. Justifies raising $50K-$100K to scale what’s working. This is “signal finding” success.

#### Scenario 3: Upside (Hope for This, Don’t Bank on It)

Probability: 10%

Ep1 Downloads: 10,000 (VTuber hit + Reddit front page)

Completion Rate: 45%

Completers: 4,500

Season Pass Conversion: 15%

Season Pass Buyers: 675

Gross Revenue: $33,750

Net Revenue: $23,625

CAC: $18/buyer ($12K ÷ 675)

LTV:CAC: 2.78:1 (sustainable)

Outcome: Significantly exceeds targets. Strong validation of product-market fit. Can proceed with confidence toward Episode 2 and Season Pass rollout.

### What $8K-$12K Can Actually Buy

#### Plausible (Well-Executed)

500-1,000 Steam wishlists over 12-week runway (mostly organic + creator)

500-800 Discord members through consistent community work

2,000-5,000 Episode 1 downloads if VTuber coverage pops

40% Episode 1 completion rate (this is quality, not budget)

Rough Math: $12K on paid UA at $2/wishlist = 6,000 “bought” wishlists. The rest must come from organic, earned, and community channels.

#### Stretch (Possible But Not Base Case)

1,000+ wishlists and 5,000+ Ep1 downloads

$23K+ VN net revenue in 90 days from standing start

15%+ Season Pass attach rate in first 30 days

LTV:CAC above 2:1 in first quarter

Reality: These require strong organic spread, VTuber hits, good timing, and product-market fit. Treat as upside, not plan.

#### Requires a “Hit” (Outlier Scenario)

2,000+ wishlists and 10,000+ Ep1 downloads in first 90 days

$50K+ VN net revenue in first quarter

LTV:CAC comfortably above 3:1 from day one

Truth: This is “Doki Doki Literature Club” / “Slay the Princess” territory. Hope for it, but don’t bet the company on it.

### Recommended Budget Framing

#### Position $8K-$12K as “Signal Finding Money”

Your main goals with this budget:
1. Prove Episode 1 quality (40%+ completion rate)
2. Find 2-3 scalable channels (VTubers, Reddit, Discord)
3. Show conversion paths (Ep1 → Season Pass at 8-12%)
4. Justify raising more capital (demonstrate unit economics improving)

NOT:
- Fully fund a sustainable revenue outcome with $8K-$12K alone
- Achieve optimal LTV:CAC in first 90 days
- Build a Kickstarter-ready community (that’s Phase 2-3 with VN revenue)

### Adjusted Expectations

#### Keep the gates as directional:

Wishlists: 500-1,000 (not 5K-10K)

Discord: 500-800 (not 1,500+)

Completion: 40% (quality gate, budget-independent)

Season Pass Conversion: 8-12% early (not 15-25%)

#### The Real Goal

Use $8K-$12K to prove the model works, then raise $50K-$100K to scale what’s working.
