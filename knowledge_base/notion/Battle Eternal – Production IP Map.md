# Battle Eternal IP Framework

High level dashboard for planning, not writing. Use each layer as a checklist of documents you maintain in the project folder before doing creative work.

[Link to Notion Outline](https://www.notion.so/IP-Production-Studio-Structural-Map-3047e5218e6c80409fbdda7539ae4929?source=copy_link)

01 · IP Spine Layer

Permanent source of truth for the universe and brand.

Core Canon

Use this layer to define the \*\*non‑negotiable\*\* rules of Battle Eternal. Nothing in other layers should contradict what lives here.

IP Bible

* Universe overview and cosmology.
* Timeline and ages (macro history).
* Factions and power systems.
* Core themes and tone guidelines.

Character Bible

* Major characters across VN, TCG, mobile.
* Archetypes and psychological drivers.
* Long‑arc plans (who changes, who stays stable).

Transmedia Map

* VN seasons and episodes as narrative spine.
* TCG product arcs and key sets.
* Mobile experiences and modes.
* Future media lanes (anime, books, etc.).

ICP & Brand Guardrails

* Mythology Seeker ICP summary.
* Non‑negotiables (completion, depth, community).
* Brand voice, tone, and visual notes.

How to use this layer

Before you outline anything, lock IP Spine assumptions.

Step 1

Create or link your IP and character bibles here, plus a one page ICP and brand guardrail summary. Every new Series, Season, or Episode must be checked against these before it is approved.

* Maintain this layer as version controlled documents in the project folder.
* Update only when you deliberately shift canon or ICP, not per episode.
* Use quick checklists here to gate creative briefs for downstream layers.

02 · Series Layer

6–10 Series roadmap for the IP.

Macro Arcs

Use this layer to map the large arcs that sit under the IP Spine. A Series is a multi‑season storyline within the shared universe.

Series Metadata

* Title and internal code name.
* Medium focus (VN core, TCG heavy, mobile, mixed).
* Target audience segment and ICP emphasis.

Series Blueprint

* Premise and core stakes.
* Position on the global timeline.
* Relation to other Series (prequel, parallel, sequel).

Season Grid

* List all seasons in this Series.
* Label function of each season (intro, escalation, payoff).

Production Notes

* Priority and release order.
* Dependencies on other Series or products.
* High level budget and risk notes.

Guidance

Think like a long term slate, not a single game.

Planning

Keep the Series layer light and visual. A grid or table is enough. The goal is to see how many big arcs you have, where they sit in time, and which mediums they lean on.

* Series = multi‑season macro story
* Bounded by IP Spine rules
* Used for investor and roadmap views

03 · Season Layer

Contained arcs within one Series.

Arc Design

Each Season is a focused narrative arc (for VN, often Episodes 1–6) with a specific emotional and thematic job inside its Series.

Season Metadata

* Season label (S1, S2, etc.).
* Episodes included (E1–E6, etc.).
* Approximate total hours and asset scale.

Concept Sheet

* Core conflict and emotional throughline.
* Key theme focus (faith, betrayal, identity, etc.).

Structure Map

* Opening state.
* Midpoint twist.
* Climax and resolution type.

Integration & Trackers

* Transmedia hooks (TCG, mobile).
* Community hooks (mysteries, theory‑crafting nodes).
* Status fields: idea, outlined, scripting, in‑engine, shipped.

Guidance

Use season docs as the bridge between vision and episodes.

Bridge

The Season layer is where you decide the emotional journey, total scope, and meta hooks. Do not write scenes here. Just define what the player should feel and learn by the end.

* Use one page per Season with fields that mirror this card.
* Link each Season back to its Series and IP Spine assumptions.

04 · Episode Layer

Repeatable unit for VN production.

Execution

Each Episode document is a production ready blueprint. Once this is stable, writers and artists can work in parallel.

Episode Metadata

* Title and short logline.
* Length target (word count, playtime).
* Production status and owner.

Design Sheet

* Narrative goal for the episode.
* Player promise (what they get by the end).
* Primary locations and new characters introduced.

Structure & Storyboard

* Beat list in order.
* Choice points and their impact size.
* Ending variants and flags (no prose, just logic).
* Scene IDs with types and visual/audio intent tags.

Tech Sheet

* Required assets (BGs, sprites, animations, SFX, music).
* Engine integration notes and complexity tags.

Guidance

Turn this into your per episode template.

Template

Mirror this card structure in a Notion page or design doc. You should be able to hand that doc to any collaborator and have them understand scope, beats, and asset needs without reading the script.

05 · Script & Prompting Layer

How you write, tag, and use AI.

Workflow

This layer standardizes how scripts are formatted and how AI is used for ideation, drafting, and revision without breaking canon or ICP.

Script Format Spec

* VN markup standard (routes, labels, flags).
* Tags for choices and conditions.
* VO, SFX, and animation call conventions.

Prompting Playbook

* Prompt patterns for dialogue drafting.
* Prompts for lore expansion and scene ideation.
* Guardrails to protect canon and ICP.

Review Flow

* Draft → human edit → lore check → ICP check → final.
* Feedback tags (tone, pacing, clarity, canon).

Tooling Notes

* Which tools you use where in the pipeline.
* Where scripts live and how they sync with engine.

Guidance

Treat this as your studio OS for words.

Standard

Finalize this layer early so writers and AI operators share the same rules. It should be easy to onboard someone into Battle Eternal scripting just by reading this spec.

06 · Studio & Roadmap Layer

Connecting IP planning to execution.

Operations

Use this layer to see the whole machine at once: which Series, Seasons, and Episodes are active, who owns them, and what needs to happen next.

Product Roadmap Grid

* Rows: Series / Seasons / Episodes.
* Columns: status, owner, target date, risk level.

Metrics & Gates

* Per Season and Episode success metrics.
* Clear gates to unlock next Season or Series.

Cross Team Views

* Narrative board.
* Art and animation board.
* Tech and engine board.
* Marketing and community board.

Cadence

* Decide how often you update the dashboard.
* Use it in weekly or biweekly studio reviews.

Guidance

Make this the single truth for planning.

Governance

This dashboard page should always match the documents in your project folder. If there is a conflict, update the docs first, then sync the dashboard.
