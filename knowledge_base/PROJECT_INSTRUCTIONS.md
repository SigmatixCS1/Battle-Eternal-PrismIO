## Canon Consistency Rules
These are non-negotiable elements of the Battle Eternal universe:

- **The Hegemony** — A dystopian one-world government using the F-Link control system for communication, food distribution, propaganda, and digital caste management.
- **F-Link** — The Hegemony's control infrastructure. Corporate hierarchy runs from F-Link up through parent corporations to the Order of the Black Sun.
- **The Furies** — Three metaphysical entities (Megaera, Tisiphone, Alethia/Alecto) who manipulate human consciousness through emotional vectors. They manage F-Link, orchestrate psychological warfare, and aim to trigger the Crimson Convergence.
- **The Crimson Convergence** — End goal of harvesting emotional energy to merge the Dark Hold with Earth's ley lines.
- **The Scapegoat Protocol** — Metaphysical karmic displacement system where elites offload Entropic Weight onto vulnerable scholarship students (Hollows) via modified F-Link connections.
- **Church of Predestination** — State religion promoting acceptance of fate, framing heroes as heretical terrorists.
- **Functionality Academies** — Education system using the Aptitude Nexus for societal role assignment.
- **Alexander Holmes Harukaza** — Protagonist with domain authority in light manipulation, truth perception, and cyberpathic systems. Has hard limits (no reality rewriting, no mind control) and a flinch enforcement mechanism against authoritarian drift.
- **Crystalis** — Trained in defensive martial art (combat Tai Chi) since age 5; abilities dramatically amplified by Athena mantle.
- **Sigmatix** — Mysterious figure who disrupts F-Link, appearing as a "blind spot" immune to observation. Carries a self-rewriting journal.

## Tone & Style Guidelines
- Battle Eternal is a **mythology-driven spy thriller** — not generic fantasy.
- Tone: serious, psychologically complex, thematically layered with mythological depth.
- Writing should balance: visceral action, emotional nuance, philosophical weight.
- Avoid: generic anime tropes, simplistic good-vs-evil framing, breaking the dystopian atmosphere.
- Target audience: Mythology Seekers — intellectually engaged consumers drawn to deep lore, archetypal storytelling, and layered world-building.
