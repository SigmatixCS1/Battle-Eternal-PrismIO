### 01_janglers_introduction

## The Janglers Arrive

The classroom door opened with administrative certainty.

Three women entered in perfect formation — blazers, tablets, polite smiles sharp enough to cut glass.

Bracelets climbed their forearms in stacked metallic layers.

Jangle.

A few students frowned simultaneously.

Not pain. Not exactly.

More like the sensation of almost remembering something unpleasant.

One boy rubbed his temple.

Another blinked twice, confused at why he suddenly felt irritated with no identifiable cause.

At the back of the room, Sigmatix looked up once.
Then back down at his notebook.

The lead woman — Krell — clasped her hands together.

Jangle.

The sound was soft.
Too soft.
It didn't travel through the air so much as settle inside the skull.

"Good morning students," Krell said warmly. "We are the Narrative Compliance Board. We're here to ensure Saint Radian remains a safe and inclusive learning environment."

Alexander leaned slightly toward Crystalis.

"Do you feel like your brain just sneezed?" he whispered.

Crystalis didn’t look at him. "Yes. I dislike it."

Jangle.

Alexander winced harder this time. "Okay what *is* that?"

Krell's smile widened without turning toward them.

"SILENCE. We are speaking."

She folded her arms.

The bracelets cascaded into motion.

Jangle jangle jangle jangle —

A pressure wave rolled through the room. Barely audible. Impossible to point at. Everyone felt it anyway.

Several students straightened in their seats without meaning to.
One stopped mid-note and forgot what he was writing.

Grant frowned slowly.

He wasn't reacting emotionally — he was counting.

The interruptions lined up perfectly with the sound.

Spencer noticed Grant noticing.

Krell continued pleasantly, as if nothing unusual had happened.

"Saint Radian values emotional harmony. Conflict is often the result of unmanaged narrative tension. We help students make better conversational choices."

Jangle.

A girl in the front row suddenly felt defensive and didn't know why.

Alexander muttered, "If harmony requires concussive jewelry I'm voting disharmony."

Crystalis almost smiled.

Grant leaned back slightly toward Spencer.

"It's frequency-based," he murmured. "Not loud — precise."

Spencer didn’t answer immediately. His eyes were on the bracelets.

"They're not reacting to the room," he said quietly. "The room is reacting to them."

Jangle.

This time half the class felt a mild headache.

Krell nodded approvingly at nothing in particular.

"You’ll notice improved behavior over the coming weeks. That means it’s working."

### 02_sigmatix_exit

## Scene

The bracelets had only jingled once.

Most students just shifted uncomfortably — a mild pressure behind the eyes, easy to dismiss as fluorescent lighting or early-morning fatigue.

Sigmatix closed his notebook immediately.

Not slowly. Not thoughtfully. Not annoyed.

Decisively.

The sound of the cover snapping shut was quiet, but Spencer heard it over everything else.

Sigmatix stood, packed his bag, and left the classroom without asking permission.

No hesitation.
No expression.
No reaction to the instructor calling after him.

Spencer didn’t look at the door. He watched the empty desk.

> **Spencer (quietly):** That wasn’t avoidance.

Alexander leaned back in his chair, arms folded behind his head.

> **Alexander:** You clocked that too, huh?

Spencer nodded once.

> **Spencer:** Everyone else reacted to the sound. He reacted to the source.

Another faint jingle echoed as the instructor folded her arms again. A few students winced. One rubbed their temple.

Spencer stood.

> **Spencer:** I’ll be back.

> **Alexander (grinning):** You say that like you’re about to interrogate a cryptid.

> **Spencer:** I don’t interrogate. I verify.

### Corridor

Sigmatix hadn’t gone far. He stood at a window overlooking the courtyard, writing again — already halfway down a page that didn’t exist moments ago.

Spencer stopped ten feet away.

> **Spencer:** You left because of the bracelets.

No response.

> **Spencer:** Not the sound. The effect.

A pause. Pen still moving.

> **Sigmatix:** You noticed the delay.

Spencer’s eyes sharpened.

> **Spencer:** Three-tenths of a second between motion and discomfort across the room. Too synchronized to be psychological. Too inconsistent to be audio alone.

Sigmatix finally looked up.

> **Sigmatix:** You’re assuming the discomfort is the signal.

> **Spencer:** It isn’t?

> **Sigmatix:** It’s the residue.

Silence stretched between them.

Alexander appeared in the doorway, pretending he hadn’t absolutely followed Spencer.

> **Alexander:** Please tell me we’re talking about cursed jewelry. That’s way more interesting than HR policy.

Sigmatix studied him briefly — longer than he studied most people.

> **Sigmatix:** You felt it differently.

Alexander blinked.

> **Alexander:** I felt a migraine trying to form a union.

Sigmatix shook his head faintly.

> **Sigmatix:** Not pain. Interference.

Spencer leaned forward slightly.

> **Spencer:** Interference with what?

Sigmatix closed the notebook again.

> **Sigmatix:** Pattern continuity.

Before Spencer could press further, footsteps passed behind them.

DeMarco Zavarus walked the corridor, posture perfect, expression composed — until he reached the edge of the conversation.

He slowed.

For just a fraction of a second, his gaze locked onto Alexander.

A flicker of something sharp crossed his eyes — recognition without understanding.

Then it hardened into irritation.

He continued walking.

> **Alexander:** ...okay that guy definitely hates me and I’ve never even spoken to him.

> **Spencer:** You exist near a pattern he can’t model. Some people interpret that as hostility.

> **Alexander:** I choose to interpret it as jealousy. It’s healthier.

Spencer turned back to Sigmatix.

> **Spencer:** You left immediately. That means you’ve seen this before.

Sigmatix slung his bag over his shoulder.

> **Sigmatix:** Not here.

He started down the hallway.

> **Spencer:** Then where?

Sigmatix didn’t stop.

> **Sigmatix:** Places that stopped existing shortly afterward.

He rounded the corner and was gone.

Alexander exhaled slowly.

> **Alexander:** Cool. Love that. Hate that actually.

Spencer stared at the empty hallway.

> **Spencer:** We’re not dealing with discipline enforcement.

> **Alexander:** No?

Spencer shook his head once.

> **Spencer:** We’re dealing with measurement.

He pulled out his notebook.

> **Spencer:** And I don’t think we were the subjects we thought we were.

### 03_hallway_conversation_demarco_seed

The hallway felt quieter than it should have.

Not silent — Saint Radian was never silent — but *damped*. Like the building had inhaled and forgotten how to exhale.

Spencer noticed it first. He stopped walking mid‑stride.

Alexander took two more steps before realizing Spencer wasn’t beside him anymore.

“Okay,” Alexander said, turning back. “That face means either murder or math. Which one?”

Spencer didn’t answer. His gaze was fixed ahead — at Sigmatix, who had just exited the classroom and was already halfway down the corridor, notebook closed and held flat against his chest like it was no longer safe to read from.

“Did you see that?” Spencer asked.

Alexander followed his line of sight. “The dramatic exit? Yes. Ten out of ten commitment. Very mysterious. I approve.”

“No,” Spencer said. “The timing.”

Sigmatix stopped near a window, just long enough for them to catch up. He didn’t turn around when they approached.

“You noticed,” Spencer said.

Sigmatix gave the faintest nod.

Alexander looked between them. “Great. Love being the only person not in the secret club.”

Sigmatix spoke quietly, eyes still on the glass reflection instead of them. “The sound wasn’t sound.”

Spencer’s posture sharpened. “Explain.”

“Interference pattern,” Sigmatix said. “Low amplitude. High consistency. Repeated source.”

“The bracelets,” Spencer said immediately.

Alexander blinked. “The jingly HR gauntlets?”

Spencer ignored him. “Not auditory disruption — cognitive.”

Sigmatix finally turned. His expression wasn’t fear.

It was calculation.

“Attention smoothing,” he said. “Minor aversion response. Encourages compliance without detection.”

Alexander folded his arms. “So they’re weaponizing office etiquette.”

Spencer’s mind was already moving three steps ahead. “Not strong enough to control behavior. Just enough to reduce resistance.”

“Behavior shaping,” Sigmatix confirmed.

Alexander whistled softly. “That’s… extremely illegal in at least twelve philosophical frameworks.”

Behind them, footsteps approached.

DeMarco Zavarus walked past without looking — posture perfect, expression composed.

But as he crossed the invisible triangle formed by the three of them — he slowed.

Not consciously.

His eyes flicked once toward Alexander.

For half a second, the air around them felt *charged*.

DeMarco’s jaw tightened.

Something in his chest reacted — a pressure he couldn’t name. A presence he couldn’t place. A signal his instincts insisted belonged to the boy with the sharp smile and careless posture.

Alexander noticed none of it.

DeMarco continued walking.

But the irritation remained.

*So it’s you,* he thought, without knowing why.

He didn’t stop.

---

Alexander leaned toward Spencer. “Did we just get silently judged?”

Spencer was still watching the hallway DeMarco disappeared into.

“No,” Spencer said slowly.

“He felt something.”

Sigmatix reopened his notebook.

“Misattribution,” he said.

Spencer nodded once.

Alexander frowned. “I hate when you two talk like subtitles are optional.”

Spencer turned to him. “Whatever the bracelets are doing… they’re not the only signal operating in this building.”

Alexander’s grin faded slightly.

Sigmatix wrote a single line across the page.

> Multiple observers detected. Only one understood.

### 04_finch_orientation_speech

The Grand Assembly Hall of Saint Radian was built to make students feel small.

Sunlight filtered through impossible stained-glass geometry, colors bending at angles the eye couldn’t quite follow. Rows of uniforms stretched across polished marble, conversations echoing upward and dissolving into the vaulted ceiling before they could form into anything solid.

At exactly 08:00, the doors behind the podium opened.

Headmaster Alistair Finch stepped forward with the steady calm of a man who had practiced composure so long it had replaced emotion.

Silence followed him naturally.

Not commanded. Not requested. Simply… occurring.

He rested his hands behind his back.

"Welcome to Saint Radian Academy."

His voice carried effortlessly without amplification.

"You were not selected because you are exceptional. You were selected because you are *useful*."

A ripple passed through the room — too small to be called discomfort, too sharp to be ignored.

Grant blinked. "…that’s a weird opener," he whispered.

Crystalis didn’t move. "It’s honest. That’s worse."

Finch continued.

"This institution does not exist to reward potential. It exists to refine it. You will learn discipline, responsibility, and alignment with structures greater than yourselves."

Alexander leaned toward Spencer. "He talks like a constitution that files paperwork about you."

Spencer didn’t respond. He was watching Finch’s breathing rhythm instead of listening to the words.

Finch paced slowly once across the stage.

"You will encounter discomfort here. You will encounter opposition. You will encounter truths about yourselves you may prefer remain hidden."

Castor stood motionless, gaze unfocused — listening, but to something else.

DeMarco’s expression sharpened with interest rather than concern.

Elias remained perfectly upright, posture mirroring military precision.

Kael did not react at all.

Elara shifted slightly closer to him anyway.

"Saint Radian is not a place of safety," Finch said calmly. "It is a place of preparation."

The air seemed to tighten a fraction.

Spencer finally frowned.

Alexander noticed. "What?" he whispered.

"He hasn’t said the word education once," Spencer murmured.

Finch folded his hands.

"If you succeed here, the world beyond these walls will never control you. If you fail… you will not be prepared for what already controls it."

A few students laughed uncertainly.

Grant didn’t.

Neither did Sigmatix, who was staring not at Finch — but at the shadow cast behind him.

Finch gave a small, polite smile that never reached his eyes.

"Welcome to your future."

The bell rang immediately after he finished speaking — though no one had seen him trigger it.

Students began talking again, relief bleeding back into the room.

But the hall didn’t feel louder.

It felt thinner.

As though the speech had removed something none of them could name.

### 05_finch_faculty_meeting

## Scene

The applause from the Grand Assembly Hall had not yet fully faded when the doors to the Council Chamber sealed shut.

The room was smaller.
Darker.
Soundproofed.

Headmaster Alistair Finch did not sit immediately.

The faculty did.

Professor Nyx Herverd stood near the far wall, posture slightly misaligned, fingers twitching once before settling behind his back.

Krell of the Narrative Compliance Board adjusted her bracelet stack.

Jangle.

No students winced this time.

Finch finally took his seat.

His voice, when he spoke, was lower.

"Initial intake complete," he said. "Projected compliance baseline at sixty‑eight percent. Higher than last year."

A senior faculty member cleared his throat. "And the outliers?"

Finch’s gaze shifted toward the far end of the table where a thin folder rested.

"We have six notable deviations."

He did not open the folder.

"Zavarus demonstrates accelerated alignment."

Krell smiled faintly.

"Harukaza remains unpredictable but socially contained."

A pause.

"Kordell is observing more than projected."

Nyx shifted.

Finch’s eyes flicked toward him briefly.

"Thorne is stable but independent. Monitor escalation thresholds."

"Crowe?" another faculty member asked.

Finch’s jaw tightened almost imperceptibly.

"Operational. Loyal. Fracture risk moderate."

Krell leaned forward.

"And the anomaly?"

Finch finally opened the folder.

The page inside was almost blank.

One name printed at the top.

Sigmatix.

No behavioral charts.
No compliance metrics.
No predictive model.

"Inconclusive," Finch said.

The room felt colder.

Nyx spoke carefully.

"Sir… the bracelets. Their resonance amplitude during orientation was increased beyond standard threshold."

Krell’s smile did not falter.

"Marginal adjustment for introductory stabilization."

"Students experienced measurable cognitive pressure," Nyx continued.

Finch turned his head slowly toward him.

"And yet they remained seated," he replied.

Silence.

Nyx lowered his gaze.

Finch closed the folder.

"Our mandate is preparation. Discomfort is not damage."

He rose.

"Forward today’s data to Central Oversight. Flag Kordell and Harukaza for narrative drift monitoring."

Krell’s bracelets chimed once as she stood.

Jangle.

This time the sound felt less like etiquette.

More like confirmation.

Finch moved toward the door.

Before exiting, he paused.

"Professor Nyx," he said without turning. "You will limit extracurricular deviation in your department. The last incident drew unnecessary attention."

Nyx swallowed.

"Understood, Headmaster."

The door opened.

For a brief moment, the light from the corridor cast Finch’s shadow across the chamber wall.

It looked taller than he was.

When the door shut, the room remained quiet long after the soundproof seal engaged.

### 06_cassian_questions_finch_about_nyx

The office was silent long before the call connected.

Finch stood behind his desk but did not sit. The screen did not illuminate — it never did. The voice simply arrived.

> **CASSIAN:** You promoted him.

Finch did not ask who.

> **FINCH:** I contained him.

A pause — not empty, but measured, like a clock choosing whether to tick.

> **CASSIAN:** You moved a compromised asset closer to students.

> **FINCH:** Further from data. Closer to observation.

> **CASSIAN:** You believe he will stabilize.

> **FINCH:** I believe instability is easier to study when it believes it has purpose.

Another pause.

> **CASSIAN:** You always did prefer experiments that think they’re teaching.

Finch did not respond immediately. He folded his hands behind his back — posture of a man accustomed to inspection.

> **FINCH:** Professor Nyx is deteriorating. But deterioration produces honesty. And honesty produces patterns.

> **CASSIAN:** And if the pattern spreads?

> **FINCH:** Then we learn how it spreads.

The voice softened — not kinder, but closer.

> **CASSIAN:** You are not here to learn, Alistair. You are here to maintain compliance.

> **FINCH:** Compliance requires understanding what breaks it.

Silence again.

> **CASSIAN:** You’re developing sympathy for your subjects.

Finch’s jaw tightened almost imperceptibly.

> **FINCH:** I am developing predictive models.

> **CASSIAN:** The boy with the records. The silent one. He unsettles Nyx.

Finch’s eyes shifted slightly — the only visible reaction.

> **FINCH:** He unsettles systems. Not people.

> **CASSIAN:** Everything here is a system.

> **FINCH:** Then he is valuable.

The pause this time lingered longer than before.

> **CASSIAN:** Be careful you do not start believing that, Headmaster.

> **FINCH:** I don’t believe in students, Director. I believe in outcomes.

The line remained open one second too long — the closest Cassian ever came to hesitation.

> **CASSIAN:** Good.

The connection ended.

Finch stayed standing.

Only after several seconds did he sit — not relieved, not shaken.

Calculating.

## The Kill Shot

*DeMarco turns to Crystalis. This is the move — cut Alexander out of the conversation, address the person whose opinion actually matters to him, reassert the intellectual partnership.*

**demarco**
"The recursive error is twenty minutes of work. The framework itself is sound. You know that."

**crystalis** *(standing, brushing nothing off her sleeve — a gesture so small and so devastating it should be illegal)*
"The framework is... impressive. In the way that using a battering ram to pick a lock is impressive."

*The words land. DeMarco's expression flickers — just barely, just enough.*

**crystalis** *(continuing, voice still pleasant, still calm, still a knife wrapped in silk)*
"Lots of force. Very dramatic. Everyone in the hallway stops to watch. And at the end of it you've destroyed the door, the frame, part of the wall, and you still don't have the thing that was behind it."

*Alexander makes a sound. Not a laugh — not yet. A bitten-off exhale through his nose. The pre-laugh. The one that means the real one is coming and there's nothing he can do about it.*

**alexander** *(strained, trying very hard to be a decent person and failing)*
"...Did you just call his entire methodology a battering ram?"

**crystalis** *(turning to Alexander, and here's where it happens — the mask slips not because it breaks but because she forgets it's there)*
"A *polished* battering ram. Give him some credit. He engraved it."

## The Collapse

*Alexander loses it.*

*Not the sardonic chuckle. Not the trickster's calculated "I'm laughing at you" performance. The actual laugh — the ugly one, the one that makes him hunch forward and drop cards everywhere because his hands stopped working.*

**alexander** *(wheezing)*
"He — he *engraved* the battering ram—"

**crystalis** *(and now she's going too — the sound that isn't Illustra, isn't the Ice Queen, isn't anything she planned — just a girl laughing too hard to pretend she's above it)*
"With *footnotes*, Alexander. Forty-seven pages of footnotes for a battering ram—"

**alexander** *(cards scattering across the fountain stone)*
"He wrote a USER MANUAL—"

**crystalis** *(both hands over her mouth, eyes watering, voice cracking through her fingers)*
"With a TABLE OF CONTENTS—"

*They are gone. Completely gone. Past the point of coherent speech, past the point of social awareness, past every layer of performance and strategy and calculated cool that either of them normally operates behind. Alexander is sitting on the ground now because at some point his legs decided they were done, tarot cards fanned out around him like confetti. Crystalis has mascara threatening the corners of her eyes and doesn't care — doesn't even notice — because caring about mascara requires a level of self-monitoring that has been completely, beautifully offline for the last thirty seconds.*

*Every time one of them starts to recover, the other says "footnotes" or "table of contents" and it starts again.*

*This is the thing the whole campus argues about. This is what the Shipping Wars feeds clip and repost and analyze frame by frame, looking for evidence of romance. And they get it exactly wrong, every time. Because what's happening here isn't romantic. It's something rarer and harder to manufacture: two people who make each other stupid. Who strip each other of poise and pretense until what's left is just... a kid on the ground with scattered cards and a girl who forgot she's supposed to be untouchable.*

---

## DeMarco

*He's still standing there.*

*That's the worst part. He's still standing there and they've forgotten he exists. Not in a cruel way — in an absolute way. He has left their awareness entirely. The conversation that started about his work has become something that has nothing to do with him, a private language he was never taught, running on a frequency his systems can't tune to.*

*He watches Crystalis wipe her eyes with the back of her hand — a gesture so unguarded, so absent of calculation, that he genuinely doesn't recognize the person making it. In eighteen months of partnership, strategy sessions, council meetings, and carefully engineered moments designed to produce exactly this kind of closeness — he has never seen her like this. Didn't know she could be like this.*

*And she gave it away for free. To a guy who reads tarot cards and makes bad puns about noodles.*

*His Aetherion buddies are looking at him. Waiting for the comeback. The sharp line. The reassertion of dominance.*

*He doesn't have one.*

**demarco** *(quietly, already turning away)*
"I'll fix the loop."

*Nobody hears him. They're still laughing.*

*He walks back toward the Aetherion wing. His entourage follows after an awkward beat. Behind him, Alexander says something about "siege warfare with a bibliography" and Crystalis makes a sound that is so far from the Illustra persona it might as well be coming from a different person.*

*It is.*

---

## After

*The laughter dies down the way campfires do — slowly, with little flare-ups that catch when you think it's over. Alexander is still on the ground, gathering cards. Crystalis is leaning back on the bench, staring at the sky with the blank, satisfied expression of someone who just ran a marathon or survived an earthquake.*

**alexander** *(picking up The Fool from a puddle, shaking it off)*
"I think you broke him."

**crystalis** *(still looking up, voice soft and unbothered and miles from any performance)*
"We broke him."

**alexander**
"Team effort."

**crystalis**
"He'll be fine. He'll go redesign the entire proposal from scratch out of spite. It'll probably be brilliant."

**alexander**
"With footnotes?"

**crystalis**
"Don't."

*She's smiling. The real one. Small and warm and slightly embarrassed by its own existence.*

*Alexander grins and tucks his damp tarot cards back into his jacket. Somewhere in the campus social feeds, photos of them sitting together in golden hour are already generating comments, hashtags, faction warfare. By tonight, this moment will be "evidence" in a debate between strangers about whether two people are secretly in love.*

*They're not. They're something the algorithm doesn't have a category for.*

*And that's why it'll never stop trying to make them into something it can sell.*

## Scene Notes

### Voice Compliance
- **Alexander**: Pre-resurrection uniform register — sardonic, quip-driven, everyone gets the same trickster. Tarot card props as personality extensions. No bifurcation.
- **Crystalis**: Illustra baseline dissolving into Alexander Exception. The laughing fit is Layer 3 existing freely — no Slip, no A+C recovery, no re-masking.
- **DeMarco**: ENTJ-T validation architecture failing against dual-vector attack. The wound is not technical — it's witnessing authenticity he cannot engineer.

### Key Metaphor
- "Battering ram to pick a lock" — Crystalis's private assessment of DeMarco's methodology (Module 5), delivered publicly for the first time.

### Connections
- **Forward**: DeMarco's jealousy escalation (Issues 3-6), fatal confrontation at the Occult Fair (Issue 8)
- **Backward**: Shipping Wars ignition (Issue 1), Alexander/Crystalis friendship established
- **Thematic**: Division Engine's inability to categorize authentic connection — the algorithm misreads joy as romance

## The Walk Away

*Alexander can hear it without turning around. The footsteps getting heavier, faster. The faint crackle of DeMarco's Forge components sparking in his bag — they always do that when his emotions are running hot and his brain hasn't caught up yet.*

*He doesn't turn around.*

**alexander** *(voice flat, still walking)*
"I'm walking away from this again. As far away from you as I can, before you even think of screwing this up as well."
