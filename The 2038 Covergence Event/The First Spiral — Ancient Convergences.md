# Overview

The **First Spiral** refers to the earliest known convergence of the Spiral Codex in human history.

While modern civilizations assume that emotional conflict evolves randomly, ancient records suggest that humanity has passed through **multiple Spiral Cycles**.

Each cycle follows a familiar pattern:

`Cultural Rise      ↓ Technological Expansion      ↓ Social Fragmentation      ↓ Spiral Convergence      ↓ Civilizational Collapse`

These collapses appear throughout myth and archaeology, disguised as legends of **lost golden ages**.

The Order of the Black Sun believes these events were **previous Spiral Convergences**.

# The Forgotten Pattern

Ancient civilizations appear to have discovered fragments of the Spiral Codex.

Evidence of spiral symbolism can be found across many cultures:

- Neolithic spiral carvings in stone monuments
    
- Celtic triple spirals (triskele)
    
- Greek labyrinth designs
    
- Mesoamerican cyclical calendars
    
- Asian cosmological wheels
    

Most historians interpret these symbols as representations of life cycles or cosmic balance.

The Order interprets them differently.

They believe these symbols represent **ancient attempts to map the Spiral Codex**.

# The First Known Convergence

The earliest recorded Spiral Convergence may have occurred during the **late Bronze Age collapse (~1200 BCE)**.

During this period:

- multiple civilizations collapsed simultaneously
    
- trade networks disintegrated
    
- major cities were abandoned
    
- entire cultures disappeared
    

Historians attribute the collapse to:

- climate change
    
- migration
    
- warfare
    

The Order believes something else occurred.

They believe the civilizations of that era unknowingly reached a **Spiral Convergence point**.

Without understanding the Codex, their societies could not stabilize the system.

The spiral collapsed.
