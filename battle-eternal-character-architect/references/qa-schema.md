# Canon and ICP QA Schema
Checklists and scoring rubrics for validating character ICP blueprints against
the Battle Eternal IP Spine, emotional safety, and narrative consistency.

## Pre-QA: IP Spine Anchors
These are non-negotiable universe truths that ALL character ICPs must respect:

### World Rules
- The Hegemony is the one-world government. It controls via F-Link.
- F-Link is a digital control system for communication, food, propaganda, and caste assignment.
- The Order of the Black Sun operates behind/within the Hegemony.
- The Furies (Megaera, Tisiphone, Alethia/Alecto) manage F-Link and orchestrate
  psychological warfare as agents of chaos within the Order.
- The Church of Predestination is the state religion promoting acceptance of fate.
- Functionality Academies use the Aptitude Nexus for societal role assignment.
- The Dark Hold is the metaphysical prison dimension for harvested souls.
- The Crimson Convergence is the endgame: merging Dark Hold with Earth's ley lines.

### Season 1 Anchors (Cognitive Dissonance Arc)
- Setting: Saint Radian Academy, 14-day structure, 12 episodes.
- The Division Engine manufactures social conflict (Shipping Wars).
- The Narrative Compliance Board (NCB) uses emotional farming tactics.
- Key events in fixed order: Centaur Incident > F-Link Symposium >
  Alexander's Death/Resurrection > Quantum Fracture > Judgment Day.
- Professor Nyx's lectures are the intellectual backbone of student awakening.
- Alexander's resurrection is caused by Nemesis, not self-willed.
- DeMarco's Quantum Fracture is accidental, caused by hubris.
- Castor's Nemesis awakening is triggered by Thomas's murder + Quantum Fracture.

### Divine Mantle Rules
- Divine mantles are inherited archetypes, not chosen powers.
- Awakening is traumatic, not triumphant.
- Each mantle comes with philosophical burden, not just power.
- Pre-awakening: gifts manifest as "supernatural intuition" the character doesn't understand.
- Post-awakening: full divine access but at severe personal cost.
- Purely human characters (Grant, Spencer, Kaelen) achieve through discipline, not divinity.
  Their humanity is a FEATURE, not a limitation.

## QA Checklist (Run After All 5 Modules)

### Identity Consistency (Module 1)
- [ ] System function is distinct from all other characters (no role overlap)
- [ ] Archetype label matches vault frontmatter or explicit override is documented
- [ ] Divine mantle interpretation is consistent with existing vault powers
- [ ] Identity pillars do not contradict vault-established behavior
- [ ] Canon-break markers are specific and actionable (not vague)
- [ ] Core Cast role is unique (no two characters share the same one-word role)

### Psychological Coherence (Module 2)
- [ ] Core wound logically produces the stated deep fear
- [ ] Surface want and true need are in genuine tension (not just restatement)
- [ ] Failure mode is specific behavioral pattern, not generic character flaw
- [ ] Key psychological event matches vault timeline (correct issue number)
- [ ] Season 1 arc shape has clear inflection point (not flat progression)
- [ ] Compression statement captures wound, fear, and growth in one paragraph

### Voice Distinction (Module 3)
- [ ] Dialogue voice is audibly distinct from every other character
- [ ] Interior thought voice differs from spoken dialogue in a defined way
- [ ] Narrator description voice creates unique "prose color" for this character
- [ ] At least 2 default tones are identified with specific trigger contexts
- [ ] At least 1 rare-event tone is identified with specific trigger
- [ ] Vocabulary USES and NEVER USES are specific enough to enforce
- [ ] Dialogue samples sound like THIS character and no one else

### Canon Integrity (Module 4)
- [ ] Power budget matches vault ability progression (no premature abilities)
- [ ] Locked powers are explicitly tied to specific arc triggers
- [ ] Knowledge state at each event is consistent with vault timeline
- [ ] No character knows information they have no narrative path to learning
- [ ] Canon-breaking behaviors align with identity pillars from Module 1

### Story Function Completeness (Module 5)
- [ ] Permanent vs. evolvable traits are clearly delineated
- [ ] Season 1 thematic job is specific to the Cognitive Dissonance Arc
- [ ] Episode-level beats reference specific issue numbers
- [ ] VN adaptation rules are actionable for a script writer
- [ ] TCG adaptation reflects character mechanical identity from vault
- [ ] Transmedia pillars are portable (would work in anime, book, game, social)

## Scoring Rubric

### Per-Module Score (1-5)
- 5: Fully locked. No ambiguity. Ready for production use.
- 4: Minor gaps. Usable but could benefit from one more pass.
- 3: Structural gaps. Key questions unanswered. Needs revision.
- 2: Major contradictions or missing sections. Cannot be used.
- 1: Not started or fundamentally misaligned with IP Spine.

### Full ICP Score
- 25/25: Production-ready. Character is LOCKED.
- 20-24: Near-complete. Address flagged gaps before locking.
- 15-19: Draft quality. Needs collaborative revision.
- Below 15: Requires restart from Module 1.

## Character-Specific Canon Risks (High-Drift Characters)

### Alexander Holmes Harukaza
- RISK: Becomes brooding/dark post-resurrection instead of humbled-but-still-catalytic.
- RISK: Helios powers used for destruction rather than revelation.
- GUARD: He must always be lighter than his circumstances suggest.

### Castor Nightfall
- RISK: Nemesis-Castor presented as villain rather than tragic instrument.
- RISK: Thomas's voice silenced or ignored in Trinity dynamics.
- GUARD: All three Trinity voices must be present in any judgment scene.

### Crystalis
- RISK: Reduced to "ice queen" stereotype without showing analytical depth.
- RISK: Athena connection turns her into exposition machine.
- GUARD: Her intellectual arrogance must be shown as a FLAW, not a feature.

### DeMarco Zavarus
- RISK: Becomes irredeemable villain rather than tragic architect.
- RISK: Guilt is forgotten or minimized after Quantum Fracture.
- GUARD: His pragmatism must always carry visible emotional cost.

### Grant Sterling
- RISK: Flattened to "nice guy support character" without agency.
- GUARD: His naivety is strength (genuine kindness), not weakness (stupidity).

### Spencer Kordell
- RISK: Becomes emotionless data machine.
- GUARD: Tragic empathy must be present in every investigation scene.

### Kaelen Thorne
- RISK: Reduced to "cool fight scenes guy" without interiority.
- GUARD: His discipline is born from trauma, not from lack of feeling.

### Elara Vance
- RISK: Becomes passive emotional support with no agency.
- GUARD: Her compassion is active choice, not passive nature. She CHOOSES to feel.

### Elias Crowe
- RISK: Presented as simple antagonist rather than conflicted enforcer.
- GUARD: His faith in the system must be shown as genuine before it shatters.

### Selene Anastas
- RISK: Prophecy becomes convenient plot device rather than burden.
- GUARD: Every vision must cost her something (dissociation, pain, guilt).

### Leone Strass
- RISK: "Dumb muscle" trope. Brutishness without inner complexity.
- GUARD: His unexplained loyalty to Castor must be treated as metaphysically significant.

### Professor Nyx Helverd
- RISK: Becomes generic "wise mentor" archetype.
- GUARD: His despair is specific -- he sees the flood and has only sand for a dam.
  He is the Doomed Messenger, not the Victorious Prophet.

## Conflict Resolution Protocol
When QA reveals contradictions between the ICP and existing vault data:
1. Present both versions to the user.
2. Ask: "Which is canon going forward?"
3. If vault data wins: adjust ICP.
4. If ICP wins: note that vault file needs updating (flag, do not auto-edit).
5. Document resolution in the ICP blueprint.
