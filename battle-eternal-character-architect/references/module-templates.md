# Module Templates
Detailed question sets and output structure for each of the 5 ICP modules.
Present questions in batches of 2-3. Pre-fill from vault data where possible.

## Module 1: Identity and System Role

### Questions to Ask
1. What function does this character serve in the Battle Eternal system?
   (Catalytic force? Structural anchor? Mirror? Accelerant? Obstacle? Witness?)
2. How does the audience primarily experience this character?
   (Through their own POV? Through others' reactions? Through consequences of their actions?)
3. What is their divine mantle or metaphysical linkage, and what does that archetype
   MEAN in the Battle Eternal system? (Not just the god's name -- how does this god's
   domain translate into narrative function?)
4. Who is this character's primary philosophical mirror? What do they share and
   where do they diverge? (Format as comparative pairs.)
5. What are 3-5 identity pillars this character MUST always embody?
   What 3-5 traits would BREAK canon if they appeared?
6. What is their one-word role in the Core Five / Core Cast?

### Output Structure
```
### Core Character System Function
[Character] is:
**[Role Label] -- [One-line function statement]**

[Character] is primarily:
- [Function 1]
- [Function 2]
- [Function 3]

### Narrative Positioning
[How audience experiences them]

### [Divine Mantle] Frame
[What the archetype means in this system]

### [Key Relationship] Dynamic
[Comparative pairs]

### Identity Pillars (Locked Canon Markers)
[Character] must always be:
- [Pillar 1-5]

[Character] is NOT:
- [Anti-pillar 1-3]

If [character] becomes those, canon breaks.

### Core Cast Role
[One-word designation]

### Module 1 Compression Statement
[Single paragraph]
```

## Module 2: Psychology, Wound and Desire

### Questions to Ask
1. What is this character's core wound? What happened (or what did they witness)
   that fundamentally shaped their worldview?
2. What is their deepest fear? Not surface-level danger -- what terrifies them
   about what they might BECOME?
3. What do they WANT on the surface vs. what do they truly NEED?
   (These should be in tension.)
4. What is their primary failure mode under stress?
   (Not a character flaw in general -- specifically how they break when pushed.)
5. What is the key psychological event in Season 1 that transforms them?
   (Death, awakening, betrayal, revelation, defection, loss?)
6. What is the psychological shape of their Season 1 arc?
   (Act I state, escalation, midpoint break, post-break state, final realization.)

### Output Structure
```
### Core Wound
[What formed them]

### Deep Fear (Locked)
[Character] is most afraid of [deepest fear].
- Not [surface fear 1].
- Not [surface fear 2].

[Character] fears:
- [Specific manifestation 1]
- [Specific manifestation 2]

### Surface Want vs True Need
Surface Want:
- [Want 1-4]

True Need:
- [Need 1-4]

### Failure Mode Under Stress
[One-word label].
Under stress, [Character]:
- [Behavior 1-4]

[Character]'s flaw is not [common misread]. It is [precise flaw].

### [Key Psychological Event]
[How this event transforms them]

### Season 1 Arc (Psychological Shape)
- Act I: [State]
- Act II: [Escalation]
- Midpoint: [Break event]
- Post-[Event]: [New state]
- Final Realization: [Thematic insight]

### Module 2 Compression Statement
[Single paragraph]
```

## Module 3: Voice, Tone and Emotional Positioning

### Questions to Ask
1. How does this character TALK in normal conversation?
   (Sentence length, formality, humor style, directness, verbal tics?)
2. How do they THINK internally? Is their inner voice different from speech?
   (More honest? More chaotic? More poetic? More calculating?)
3. How should a NARRATOR describe them in prose or VN text?
   (What emotional color does the prose take on when focused on them?)
4. What are their 2-4 default tonal registers?
   (e.g., sardonic + measured + curious. Or: cold + precise + occasionally warm.)
5. What tones are "rare events" -- only surfacing in extreme moments?
   (e.g., genuine tenderness, uncontrolled rage, desperate pleading.)
6. What words, phrases, or metaphors does this character consistently USE?
   (Domain-specific vocabulary, recurring images, verbal habits.)
7. What words, phrases, or metaphors does this character NEVER use?
   (What would sound wrong in their mouth?)

## Module 4: Canon, Power and Knowledge Constraints

### Questions to Ask
1. What can this character DO in Season 1, issue by issue?
   (Map power acquisition to specific narrative beats.)
2. What is explicitly OFF-LIMITS until a later arc or trigger event?
   (Powers, knowledge, relationships, transformations.)
3. At each key event, what does this character KNOW and NOT KNOW?
   (Centaur Incident, F-Link Symposium, Death/Resurrection, Quantum Fracture, Judgment Day.)
4. What behaviors or outcomes would BREAK CANON for this character?

   deliberate, written turning point justifies the shift.)

## Module 5: Story Function Across Layers

### Questions to Ask
1. In the IP Spine (permanent universe level), what about this character can
   NEVER change vs. what is allowed to evolve?
2. In Season 1 specifically, what is this character's emotional and thematic JOB
   in the Cognitive Dissonance Arc?
3. In episodes where this character is foregrounded, what MUST they achieve or
   reveal? (List specific episode beats.)
4. How does this character's voice and function adapt across media?
   - Visual Novel: How do they speak in dialogue boxes? What choices involve them?
     What does the player learn through their scenes?
   - TCG: What does their card text sound like? What mechanics reflect their identity?
   - Community: If this character posted on social media in-world, what would they say?
   - Transmedia: What 2-3 voice traits MUST hold no matter the format?

### Output Structure
```
### IP Spine Layer
PERMANENT (never changes):
- [Trait 1]
- [Trait 2]

ALLOWED TO EVOLVE:
- [Trait 1]
- [Trait 2]

### Season 1 Thematic Job

- [Function 1]
- [Function 2]

### Episode-Level Function

- Issue [X]: Must [achieve/reveal] [specific beat]
- Issue [X]: Must [achieve/reveal] [specific beat]
```

## Narrator Voice (Special Module)
The Narrator is treated as a 13th entity with a modified module set:
- Module 1: Narrative identity (omniscient? limited? unreliable? shifting?)
- Module 2: Emotional positioning (neutral? invested? poetic? clinical?)
- Module 3: Voice rules (vocabulary, sentence structure, tone shifts per character focus)
- Module 4: Knowledge constraints (what the narrator reveals vs. withholds per issue)
- Module 5: Cross-media adaptation (VN narrator box rules, TCG narrator text, community voice)
