The Grand Assembly Hall of Saint Radian was built to make students feel small.

Sunlight filtered through impossible stained-glass geometry, colors bending at angles the eye couldn’t quite follow. Rows of uniforms stretched across polished marble, conversations echoing upward and dissolving into the vaulted ceiling before they could form into anything solid.

At exactly 08:00, the doors behind the podium opened.

Headmaster Alistair Finch stepped forward with the steady calm of a man who had practiced composure so long it had replaced emotion.

Silence followed him naturally.

Not commanded. Not requested. Simply… occurring.

He rested his hands behind his back.

"Welcome to Saint Radian Academy."

His voice carried effortlessly without amplification.

"You were not selected because you are exceptional. You were selected because you are *useful*."

A ripple passed through the room — too small to be called discomfort, too sharp to be ignored.

Grant blinked. "…that’s a weird opener," he whispered.

Crystalis didn’t move. "It’s honest. That’s worse."

Finch continued.

"This institution does not exist to reward potential. It exists to refine it. You will learn discipline, responsibility, and alignment with structures greater than yourselves."

Alexander leaned toward Spencer. "He talks like a constitution that files paperwork about you."

Spencer didn’t respond. He was watching Finch’s breathing rhythm instead of listening to the words.

Finch paced slowly once across the stage.

"You will encounter discomfort here. You will encounter opposition. You will encounter truths about yourselves you may prefer remain hidden."

Castor stood motionless, gaze unfocused — listening, but to something else.

DeMarco’s expression sharpened with interest rather than concern.

Elias remained perfectly upright, posture mirroring military precision.

Kael did not react at all.

Elara shifted slightly closer to him anyway.

"Saint Radian is not a place of safety," Finch said calmly. "It is a place of preparation."

The air seemed to tighten a fraction.

Spencer finally frowned.

Alexander noticed. "What?" he whispered.

"He hasn’t said the word education once," Spencer murmured.

Finch folded his hands.

"If you succeed here, the world beyond these walls will never control you. If you fail… you will not be prepared for what already controls it."

A few students laughed uncertainly.

Grant didn’t.

Neither did Sigmatix, who was staring not at Finch — but at the shadow cast behind him.

Finch gave a small, polite smile that never reached his eyes.

"Welcome to your future."

The bell rang immediately after he finished speaking — though no one had seen him trigger it.

Students began talking again, relief bleeding back into the room.

But the hall didn’t feel louder.

It felt thinner.

As though the speech had removed something none of them could name.
