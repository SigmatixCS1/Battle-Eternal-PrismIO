# Scene — The Corridor Fracture

## Setting

**show corridor_east_wing_night**
**play sound fluorescent_hum pipes_dripping**

*The East Wing maintenance corridor. Exposed pipes, flickering industrial lighting, unpainted concrete — the skeleton of Saint Radian that the polished facades are designed to hide. Students aren't supposed to be here. It's the gap between the Aetherion labs and the old infrastructure wing, and it smells like damp metal and cleaning solution.*

*Alexander walks with purpose. Not rushing — just done. The kind of stride that says the conversation ended before it started. His tarot deck sits warm in his jacket pocket. The Cyberpathy he's still learning to control picks up ambient F-Link traffic through the corridor's buried wiring like tinnitus he can never fully turn off.*

*DeMarco follows. Not matching pace. Gaining.*

---

## The Walk Away

*Alexander can hear it without turning around. The footsteps getting heavier, faster. The faint crackle of DeMarco's Forge components sparking in his bag — they always do that when his emotions are running hot and his brain hasn't caught up yet.*

*He doesn't turn around.*

**alexander** *(voice flat, still walking)*
"I'm walking away from this again. As far away from you as I can, before you even think of screwing this up as well."

*The fluorescent light above him flickers. Not the building's fault.*

*He keeps walking. Every part of him that came back from dying remembers exactly what it felt like. The temperature of it. The sound of DeMarco's voice right before it happened. You don't forget that kind of thing. You just learn to walk away from the person who did it, and you learn to do it faster each time.*

---

## The Close

**play sound footsteps_aggressive echo_concrete**

*DeMarco closes the distance in four strides. His shadow swallows Alexander's under the overhead light.*

*The thing about DeMarco is that his brain works faster than anyone's — except when it doesn't. And it doesn't work when someone isn't playing the game. His whole system is built on competition: measure the threat, outperform the threat, reassert dominance. It needs a rival. It needs Alexander to care.*

*Alexander isn't caring. Alexander is walking away like DeMarco is a puddle he's stepping around.*

*That's what breaks it.*

**demarco** *(voice raw, rising, closing the space between them)*
"You really think you're better than me, don't you?!?"

*The words hit the concrete walls and bounce back. They sound enormous and pathetically small at the same time.*

---

## The Cut

*Alexander stops.*

*Not because DeMarco reached him. Because something behind his ribs answered first — something bright and heavy that wasn't there before he died.*

*He turns. And whatever DeMarco expected to see on his face — anger, contempt, the old trickster smirk — it isn't there. What's there instead is worse. It's the look of someone who used to find you interesting and doesn't anymore.*

**alexander** *(low, quiet, the kind of quiet that fills a room instead of emptying it)*
"I don't think about you at all."

*Beat.*

*DeMarco's mouth opens. Nothing comes out. His brain is cycling — trying to find the framework, the counter-argument, the logical restructure that turns this into something he can win. But his system was built to process rivalry. Competition. Threat assessment. It was not built for this: the discovery that he takes up no space in Alexander's head.*

*Indifference isn't an attack. It's an absence. And you can't outperform an absence.*

*Alexander holds the look for exactly two seconds. Then something shifts — not softer, just heavier. Like the air in the corridor decided to pay attention.*

**alexander** *(each word unhurried, precise, carrying weight he doesn't entirely control)*
"Except to stay well away from you. Cuz you're an absolute liability."

*He can feel it — the thing behind his ribs pressing forward, wanting to say more. Wanting to lay it all out: You killed me. You lied about it. You broke reality because you couldn't handle losing, and every fix you've built since then has made it worse. You are not my enemy. You're not even my problem. You're a cautionary tale I carry in my chest where a heartbeat used to be.*

*He doesn't say any of that. Something catches — a kind of internal flinch, like touching a stove — and he swallows the rest of it.*

*He turns away. Starts walking again.*

---

## DeMarco — Alone

**show corridor_empty_flickering**
**play sound fluorescent_buzz distant_hum_fading**

*DeMarco stands in the corridor.*

*His Forge components have gone completely still. Not sparking, not crackling. Silent, like they hit something they couldn't process and just... stopped.*

*His hands are shaking. Not from anger. From the aftershock of reaching for a framework and finding nothing there.*

*Because if Alexander genuinely doesn't think about him — if the rivalry that drove DeMarco to touch the Source, to break reality, to kill someone over a girl who isn't even his — if all of that was one-sided...*

*Then he's been fighting a war that only he showed up to.*

*He looks down at his hands. The faint glow of ionic burns from his latest Forge session. The same hands that channeled the Source. That tore the sky. That aimed a killing spell at a classmate during the Occult Fair and told himself afterward it was an accident.*

*The guilt, for one terrible moment, has nothing to hide behind.*

**demarco** *(barely audible, to no one)*
"...Liability."

*The fluorescent light above him pops — a sharp, bright death — and the corridor goes half-dark.*

*He stands in it.*

*Behind him, the sound of Alexander's footsteps fading. Steady. Unhurried. The walk of someone who decided a long time ago that this conversation was over, and is just now getting around to leaving it.*

*DeMarco doesn't follow.*

*There's nowhere to go when the person you built yourself against stops being a mirror and becomes a wall you're talking to alone.*

**fade to black**

## Scene Notes

### Voice Compliance
- **Alexander**: Post-resurrection split register — darker, more direct toward antagonists. No trickster quips. No tarot puns. The playful rivalry is dead. What replaced it is worse: disappointment delivered as diagnosis. The Flinch activates — he feels the Helios register pressing to say more and catches himself. Safeguard holds.
- **DeMarco**: Cognitive Shifting fails against a category it wasn't built for. He can process competition, threat, even hatred. He cannot process irrelevance. The ENTJ-T "allergic to appearing weak" reflex drives the physical aggression — closing space when losing verbal ground.

### Key Shift
- Module 5 executed: "Post-resurrection, Alexander shifts from provocation to disappointment. That shift creates asymmetrical tension. Alexander grows. DeMarco festers."
- Pre-resurrection Alexander would have answered with a tarot pun and a holographic prank. Post-resurrection Alexander walks away. The asymmetry is the wound.

### Connections
- **Backward**: Alexander's death at the Occult Fair (Issue 8), resurrection (Issue 9), DeMarco's lies to the academy
- **Forward**: DeMarco's spiral toward the Quantum Fracture, the guilt that drives his countermeasure obsession
- **Contrast**: Test Scene 07 (Courtyard Smoke) — same rivalry, completely different Alexander. The distance between the two scenes is the distance death put between them.
