# Battle Eternal — Mantle Taxonomy

## Overview

Divine Mantles are stabilized archetypal identities within the Dreamfield that can attach themselves to compatible human hosts.

Mantles are not literal gods but mythic identity frameworks formed through centuries of collective belief.

When a human host awakens a mantle, they become the current vessel through which the archetype expresses itself in the physical world.

---

## Mantle Classes

### Solar Mantles

Archetype: illumination, sovereignty, revelation.

Solar mantles expose hidden truths and destabilize deception.

Examples: Helios, Amaterasu.

---

### Judgment Mantles

Archetype: justice, consequence, karmic balance.

These mantles enforce cosmic accountability.

Examples: Nemesis, Ma’at.

---

### War Mantles

Archetype: conflict, conquest, martial power.

War mantles escalate conflicts to mythic scale.

Examples: Ares, Hachiman.

---

### Trickster Mantles

Archetype: disruption, paradox, transformation.

Trickster mantles destabilize rigid systems and expose hidden weaknesses.

Examples: Hermes, Loki.

---

### Underworld Mantles

Archetype: death, memory, transformation.

These mantles govern the relationship between life and the afterlife.

Examples: Hades, Izanami.

---

### Nature Mantles

Archetype: wilderness, instinct, fertility.

Nature mantles bridge the gap between pantheon archetypes and the Primal Wilds.

Examples: Artemis, Inari.

---

### Sovereignty Mantles

Archetype: leadership, rulership, destiny.

These mantles embody mythic authority and command.

Examples: Zeus, Odin.

---

## The Mantle Index

The Order of the Black Sun tracks awakened hosts through a classification system called the Mantle Index.

Each host receives a coded designation based on archetypal type.

Examples:

SOL-01 → Solar Mantle Host  
JUD-01 → Judgment Mantle Host  
WAR-02 → War Mantle Host

---

## Narrative Function

The Mantle Taxonomy ensures that divine archetypes emerge in a structured and predictable way as the Dreamfield destabilizes.

This system explains the rise of pantheon hosts leading into the Pantheon War.
